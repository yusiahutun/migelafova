New virus blamed for killing 31 snakes at Ocean Park

27 Apr 2024, 00:00

A new virus has been identified as the cause of an illness that killed 31 baby anacondas at Ocean Park three years ago. A study, led by University of Hong Kong microbiologist Yuen Kwok-yung, found the virus that killed the newborn anacondas was closely related to other reptilian paramyxovirus, but it was capable of being transmitted only among snake species. The report was published in the last week. The epidemic in 2011 was believed to have originated from a mother snake bought from Japan, which was found to be pregnant only after arriving at Ocean Park in Wong Chuk Hang.
