    Nigella Lawson shares delicious two-ingredient pasta sauce perfect for a quick meal

    09 Apr 2024, 00:00

    For many, pasta dishes are the go-to meal when in a rush, on a budget or simply not up for cooking.

    But, while you may be tempted to speed up the process even more by buying ready-made sauces, they often have far more sugar in than homemade recipes.

    Nigella Lawson has come to our rescue, though. In her latest episode of Cook, Eat, Repeat, she shared a simple recipe to make a delicious ‘Nduja pasta sauce using just two ingredients.

    To begin with, the celebrity chef melts a large knob of butter in a wok.

    “I used butter here rather than, say, oil because its sweetness really seems to balance the fabulous fieriness of the ‘Nduja,” she says.

    “And more, it seems as well to help thicken the sauce.”

    All that is needed to finish off the sauce is a couple of “splodges” of ‘Nduja paste – which can be easily bought on Amazon for £8.49.

    Nigella mixed the two ingredients together to form a thick red sauce before adding her pasta.

    The 50-year-old opts to use fusiloni – effectively extra-large fusilli – for her meal.

    She had earlier boiled them with potatoes and Tuscan kale but the sauce will likely go with most pastas.

    With the incredibly simple meal finished, Nigella says: “When the skies are dark and the air chilled, this really bolsters and brightens.

    “There may be a faster pasta sauce but I haven’t encountered it.”

    It comes after Nigella shared her unusual cooking hack to make an ordinary chili con carne taste even more delicious.
