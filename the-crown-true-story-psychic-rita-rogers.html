    The true story of Rita Rogers, Princess Diana's psychic

    28 Mar 2024, 00:00

    One of the most exciting TV drops of the autumn is landing tomorrow: the first instalment of season six of The Crown, all four episodes of which will be available from 16 November on Netflix. This time, the true-to-life drama following the trials and tribulations of the royal family will chart Prince Charles and Princess Diana's first summer as a divorced couple, with Diana being courted by the Fayeds and Charles furthering his relationship with Camilla Parker-Bowles.

    The hotly anticipated series has already generated plenty of discussion, thanks to an updated cast (including a young Kate Middleton and Prince William at St Andrews University) and the sneak peeks we've been given of Elizabeth Debicki's looks as Diana – her swimsuits, in particular, are dead ringers for the Nineties styles favoured by the Princess.

    Keith Bernstein//Netflix

    Elizabeth Debicki as Diana in the latest instalment of the hit show

    From paparazzi intrusion to Balmoral traditions, the episodes are a snapshot into the lives of the royals at that time. But one element, in particular, has caught our attention: a visit made by Diana and Dodi to a psychic, Rita Rogers, just weeks before they died in that fatal car crash in Paris.

    "[There was this] amazing scene where Dodi and Diana take a helicopter to go and see a woman who was a psychic, who Diana was very close to," says Debicki, revealing that she even took a few of the psychic's business card as a memento from the set. "The woman playing her was so amazing, and I spied a bunch of business cards on the table, so I snapped some of them up. [They're] in my wallet – Rita Rogers. That's the only little bit of memorabilia I took."

    Netflix

    Diana and Dodi in the latest season of The Crown

    But did Dodi and Diana really visit Rita Rogers, and why?

    Who was Rita Rogers?

    A medium of Romany origin, Rita Rogers (who is now 82 years old) had many famous and noteworthy clients on her books, including the police, and most notably Princess Diana. In 1997, Diana and Dodi flew by helicopter to Lower Pilsley in Derbyshire for an appointment with Rogers, to whom Diana had been introduced by a mutual friend in 1994. Diana had already visited Rogers twice before, and is believed to have telephoned her regularly.

    "She rang at least once a week to see how I was, as well as for readings," Rogers told the Derbyshire Times. "When she came to visit, she used to throw her arms around me," she added in another interview. "She always used to leave me a present... we had such laughs and such a fantastic relationship."

    Kevin Holt/Daily Mail/Shutterstock

    Rita Rogers, pictured outside her home in Lower Pilsley in 1997

    What was her meeting with Dodi and Diana about?

    The last time Diana would see Rogers was on the 12 August 1977, for a half-hour appointment with Dodi at the medium's house. Apparently, Dodi had been so impressed by Rogers' accurate readings, that he was keen to fly to Derbyshire for his own meeting.

    "I'd told her she would meet a man of foreign descent with the initial 'D' on water – and that the man would be connected with the film industry," Rogers said. "Not long after, she rang me one day and said, 'Rita, guess where I am? I'm on a boat with a man I've just met called Dodi Fayed'." Dodi was so amazed by the prediction that he wanted to meet Rogers himself.

    Michel Dufour//Getty Images

    Dodi and Diana, with a young Prince William, on holiday in the South of France in 1997 – shortly before their visit to Rita Rogers

    "Diana had visited several times before on her own, and we spoke regularly as friends," Rogers said in an interview with the Daily Mail. "I'd read for her several times, of course, but not on this occasion. She wanted me to meet Dodi, and while I did a private reading for him, she sat in the sunshine on the patio with [my husband] until we had finished."

    It seems the visit left a big impression – Diana gave Rogers a 15-carat gold necklace from Van Cleef & Arpels, one of her favourite jewellers, after their visit, with a personal note: "This necklace was made for you and comes with so much love to a very special lady, with an extraordinary gift..."

    Elizabeth Debicki as Diana in the latest season of The Crown

    Has Rogers ever revealed any details about her meetings with Diana?

    Rogers has been tight-lipped about much of what was discussed between her and the princess. "I was offered £250,000 to talk about her private life, but how could I? She was my friend," she said. She has, however, shared some small details which may give an insight into Diana's life at the time.

    She believes that "the crash was an accident – and no, she wasn't pregnant as people have said." She also added that Diana and Dodi were "very happy; it warmed your heart to see them together. Would they have married? I don't know, but I do know that Diana would never have done anything that would have caused a problem for her sons. They were her absolute priority. But there's also no doubt that they were in love. She was radiant, the happiest I'd ever known her."

    Rogers claims that her connection to Diana is not lost, even today. "I still feel her around me – she's happy, and she's proud of her boys," she says.

    Getty

    A young Diana with her sons

    Did she predict Princess Diana's death?

    In her interview with the Daily Mail, Rogers relates how she warned Dodi about the accident – "specific details of the colour of the car, the tunnel, and told him to always use his own driver" – words that were, ultimately, to prove fatal when the pair died in Paris just weeks later.

    Rogers says that she did not, however, predict that Diana would be with him at the time. "She rang me at 4pm, nine hours before the crash, to tell me she was in Paris," she recounted, adding that "she told me she was going for dinner at the Ritz, that she would be careful, and would ring me when she got back home. She said she was looking forward to seeing her boys. In that last phone call to me, I told her I wanted her to come home straight away, rather than in the dark."

    Later that night, Rogers woke up to see on the TV that Dodi had died in a car crash. "I rang [Diana's] mobile, but I only got her voicemail," she said. "I have helped to save so many lives in the past by giving warnings in time. I don't understand why I couldn't save hers."
