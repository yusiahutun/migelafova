    Is karma biblical? Karma is a theological concept found in the Hindu and Buddhist religions. It is the idea that how you live your life will determine the impact of life you will have after reincarnation (reborn into a new earthly).

    Karma is based on the divine belief in reincarnation. The Bible rejects the idea of reincarnation; hence, it does not support the idea of karma.

    Bible verses about getting what you deserve

    The Bible is filled with verses discussing the concept of receiving what one deserves based on one's actions. Here are verses that focus on the balance between one's deeds and their consequences:

    • But whoever has doubts is condemned if he eats because the eating is not from faith. For whatever does not proceed from faith is a sin. Roman 14:13
    • I, the Lord, search the heart and examine the mind to reward each person according to their conduct, according to what their deeds deserve. Jeremiah 17:10
    • God will repay each person according to what they have done. Romans 2:6
    • For we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, so that each of us may receive what is due us for the things done while in the body, whether good or bad. 2 Corinthians 5: 10
    • And with you, Lord is unfailing love, and you reward everyone according to what they have done. Psalm 62:12
    • A wicked person earns deceptive wages, but the one who sows righteousness reaps a sure reward. Proverbs 11:18
    • Look, I am coming soon. My reward is with me, and I will give to each person according to what they have done. Revelation 22:12

    Bible quotes about karma

    The Bible doesn't talk about karma but discusses the idea of reaping what you sow. It means that the outcomes you experience are often a result of your actions, whether good or bad. Below are quotes about Karma from the Bible:

    • For according to the work of a man, he will repay him, and according to his ways, he will make it befall him. Job 34:11
    • Whoever seeks righteousness finds favour, but evil comes to one who searches for evil. Proverbs 11:27
    • The trouble caused by the sinners draws back on them; their violence comes down on their heads. Psalm 7:16
    • And the sinners will go away into everlasting punishment, but the moral will go into eternal life. Matthew 25:46
    • Whoever believes in the Lord has eternal life, but whoever rejects Him will not see life, for God's anger remains on them. John 3:36
    • Therefore, they must eat the bitter fruit of living their own way, choking on their own schemes. Proverb 1:31

    Bible verses about what goes around comes back around

    What does the Bible say about what goes around comes around? Reading the Bible verses gives the wisdom of knowing the difference between good and bad. The following verses teach about what goes around comes back around:

    • Don't be deceived, as God can't be mocked. A man reaps what he sows. Galatians 6:7
    • As I have seen, those who cultivate iniquity and sow trouble reap the same. Job 4:8.
    • Whoever excavates a pit will fall into it if someone else rolls a stone. Proverbs 26:27
    • So do to others what you would like to be done unto you, for this sums up the law and prophets. Matthew 7:12
    • Anyone who sows injustice will reap calamity, and the rod of his jury will fall. Proverbs 22:8

    Final word

    Is karma a sin in the Bible? While the term karma isn't found in the Bible, the principle of harvesting what you sow is an illustrated concept throughout its pages. Bible verses about karma stress the importance of your actions and decisions. They emphasize the role of grace and forgiveness in your lives and reassure people of God's endless mercy and love.
