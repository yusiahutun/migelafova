    Thousands of Kenyans have thronged Kasarani Stadium to witness President-elect William Ruto's inauguration as the fifth head of state.

    In anticipation of being part of history, Kenyans started arriving at the venue as early as 3am and by 5am, the police said the stadium was already filled to capacity.

    Consequently, in a statement on Tuesday, September 13, police spokesperson Bruno Shioso implored Kenyans to make alternative arrangements to view the proceedings, especially from the comfort of their homes.

    A spot check by TUKO.co.ke established that thousands of people had been locked out of the stadium, to their disappointment.

    Stampede

    In other news, over five people were injured in a stampede at the Kasarani as Kenyans forced their way into the venue to witness Ruto's swearing-in.

    In the incident, while screaming hysterically, the anxious attendees overwhelmed the security officers managing the stadium's gate and broke it.

    Others breached the five-meter fence and trooped into the venue without being frisked.

    In the event, several people sustained injuries and were evacuated from the scene by the Kenya Red Cross personnel.

    Mama Ruto arrives

    As reported, Ruto's mother, Sarah Cheruiyot, proved to be great to her family.

    Mama Sarah, arrived at her son's swearing-in ceremony at the Kasarani Stadium on September 13, 2022, accompanied by a few family members.

    She wore a sky blue outfit, a white hat and black flat shoes. The elderly mother walked majestically on the red carpet, making her way into the stadium as she waved to journalists.
