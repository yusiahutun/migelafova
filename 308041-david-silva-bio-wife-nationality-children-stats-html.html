    David currently plays in the position of supporting striker for the Manchester City football club. However, he can also function well as a second striker or winger. The Spanish professional footballer possesses a unique ability to pass, possess, and retain the ball when on the pitch. Here are some interesting facts about the renowned attacking midfielder that you need to know about.

    David Silva biography

    David Silva was born on 8th January 1986 at a place called Arguineguin in Spain. His parents are Fernando Jimenez and Eva. His father is of Spanish decent and a former municipal police officer and footballer while his mother is of Japanese descent. David's siblings are Nando Jimenez and Natalia Jimenez. The renowned midfielder had a humble beginning in the Spanish town of Arguineguin.

    From an early age, David had an interest in playing football. At the age of just two years, Silva would be seen kicking oranges and potatoes in front of their house. To get rid of this fruit-kicking behaviour, his parents bought him a ball at the age of three years. David was determined to make it in football from an early age. He broke his arm after being hit with a ball, but this did not deter him from playing. It is because of this determination that his parents made the decision to enroll him in San Fernando Academy when he was eight years old so that he would kick start his career in football.

    David Silva wife and son

    David Silva is not married, but he has a girlfriend, Jessica Suarez. The two have a son named Mateo who was born prematurely in January 2018 and struggled to stay alive. He was forced to stay in the hospital for five months until May when he was discharged. This was a very tough moment for David Silva as he barely slept or ate. He had to travel back and forth from Spain to be with his girlfriend and son. During this period, he could hardly train, and this forced him to miss out on a number of games. On 20th August 2018, he stole the show when he introduced his baby boy to the Manchester City fans at Etihad after the 6-1 victory against Huddersfield.

    Football is something that David Silva had a passion for right from when he was old enough to kick a ball. His football career began in the year 1995 when he joined San Fernando Academy. He initially took on the role of the goalkeeper before turning into a winger. He stayed in the academy for five years before proceeding to Valencia Academy at the age of 14 years. He would, later on, accept the offer to play for the youth wing of Valencia club. In 2003, he was asked to sign a professional contract with the club. After the deal, David was sent to the reserve team. He made only 12 appearances and scored a single goal for the club in the period between 2003 and 2004.

    His performance as a member of the reserve team was exceptional, and he was promoted to join the first team. In the year 2005, David was sent to play for the Eibar club on loan. He managed to score five goals in 35 matches during his tenure at the club. The same year, he was also sent to Celta de Vigo club on loan. David was one of the key players of this new club, and he managed to score four goals in 34 matches. David became one of the pivot players of Valencia club following the steady performance that he exhibited when playing for the temporary clubs. In the period between 2005 and 2010, he managed to score 21 goals in 119 matches. During the 2008-2009 La Liga season, David’s performance was exceptional as he managed to score eight goals for the Valencia team.

    David as the pivot player of Manchester City

    In the year 2010, David accepted the offer to play for the Manchester City club on a four-year contract. The transfer cost to this club remains undisclosed. His debut in the Premier League came in August 2014 when he appeared in the match against Tottenham Hotspur. He scored the first goal for Manchester City on 16th September when the team played against Red Bull Salzburg. The impressive performance of David would see him earn the coveted “Manchester City Player of the Month” award for three months in a row in the period between November and December 2010.

    During the 2011-2012 season, David continued with the impressive performance and was named as the man of the match during the match between Manchester City and Swansea City as well as the Bolton Wanderers teams. The remarkable display on the field would see him earn another coveted title of the “Premier League Player of the Month” in October 2011.

    David as a key player of the Spain National Football team

    In Spain, David is one of the key players of the national football team. In the period between 2001 and 2006, he played for the U-16 and U-21 teams. During the FIFA Under-17 world championships that were held in 2003 in Finland, David managed to net three goals. He scored a total of four goals during the FIFA under-21 World Youth Championships and was named one of the highest goal scorers. He has made 64 appearances for the senior team and managed to score 18 goals. The most important goal that he has scored for the team is the opening goal in the final game of Spain against Italy in the year 2012. Silva has helped the national team win the UEFA Euro in 2008 and 2012. He also helped the team win the FIFA World Cup that was held in 2010.
