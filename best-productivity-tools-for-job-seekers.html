    The 6 Best Productivity Tools for Job Seekers

    Finding relevant jobs and filling out lengthy application forms can be stressful. Manually doing all of this can become tedious at times. The process can be highly frustrating and takes up a lot of your valuable time.

    05 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Finding relevant jobs and filling out lengthy application forms can be stressful. Manually doing all of this can become tedious at times. The process can be highly frustrating and takes up a lot of your valuable time.

    We have compiled a list of six tools below to assist you in the job-seeking and application process. Some of these tools will filter the right jobs for you, while others will significantly reduce the time it takes to fill out your job applications. So, no matter what profession you're in, give these tools a spin.

    1. Jobscan

    Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems to screen out the relevant candidates based on their resumes. Thus, optimizing your resume directly impacts the chance of being shortlisted by an applicant tracking system (ATS) so a human can further screen you.

    Jobscan can help you optimize your resume for Applicant Tracking Systems based on the job description you are applying for. All you need to do is upload your resume and the description of the job you're applying for.

    With Jobscan, you can scan your resume for hard skills, education level, job title, soft skills, and other related keywords and find out where you might be lacking and what the match rate is.

    RELATED: How to Optimize Your LinkedIn Profile to Get More Clients

    As Jobscan recommends aiming for a match rate of at least 80 percent, add relevant hard skills and keywords until you reach this goal. Following every scan, you'll find many tips that will help you improve your match rate.

    By the time the match rate of your resume reaches 80 percent, you have a high chance of passing the initial screening by the applicant tracking system. If you're applying for a new job, make sure you first scan your resume in Jobscan.

    2. Mailtrack.io

    Job seekers who frequently apply via email will find Mailtrack useful. It tracks email activity and lets you know whether an email has been opened by the recruiter or not. You can then decide whether to expect a response from them or move on to applying for another position.

    The tool is free to use, and you can turn on real-time notifications and receive emails whenever one of the recruiters opens your email. The information you provide to Mailtrack is safe and secure, and Mailtrack guarantees not to sell the information to third parties.

    Download: Mailtrack for Chrome (Free)

    3. My Interview Practice

    My Interview Practice is an interview simulator platform holding mock interviews for more than hundreds of job professions. The questions are pre-fed, so you can conduct the interviews whenever it is convenient for you.

    Unlike text-based interviews where you can only read the questions and answer them, the website asks you for access to your camera and microphone to make the interview more realistic. It also helps in reducing the pressure during your real interview.

    In addition, your entire interview is recorded while you're busy responding to questions. Afterward, you can check it to see how well you've performed and filter out the areas that need improvement.

    Whether you're a health professional, an IT expert, a graduate in engineering, or an artist, you'll find a sample interview on the website. Thus, you will not have to ask your friend to spare time to practice your interview.

    If you're going to interview for your first job, want to overcome your fears, or would like to improve your interview skills, My Interview Practice is a great place to start.

    4. Shapr

    Shapr is an excellent resource for meeting new people if you lack networking skills. Shapr uses machine-learning to find 15 people with the same interests as you every day, increasing your chances of making a lasting connection. Unlike LinkedIn, Shapr eliminates the chaos.

    RELATED: Tips to Speed Up Searching for Jobs on Indeed

    By knowing that the person you're contacting has similar interests to yours, you won't have to wait hours for a reply, as with LinkedIn, where you may not even receive a response from the person.

    Some of your connections may also include job seekers like you, but having a solid relationship for future references is well worth it. Apart from the aspiring professionals, you can make new friends and enjoy a cup of coffee on a Sunday evening. In your job search, Shapr can help you expand your network.

    5. LiveCareer Apply

    Filling out job application forms every so often becomes monotonous. We usually refrain from applying to many great opportunities due to lengthy application forms. A single protracted application form could have landed you your dream job. However, that won't happen unless you have enough time to fill it up.

    The LiveCareer Apply extension simplifies the online application process. Once you enter your basic information, it retains it and automatically fills out your job application form next time.

    It integrates with most online job portals, so it comes in handy. These sites include ZipRecruiter, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, CareerBuilder, Indeed, Monster, Snagajob, Dice, LinkUp, SimplyHired, USAJobs, WayUp, and many others.

    You need to press one button to fill out your form, recheck the auto-filled information, make any minor changes, and boom! You're one step closer to landing your dream job. In addition to making things easier, it saves you time, so you can find other opportunities. If you hate filling out application forms all the time, try LiveCareer Apply.

    Download: LiveCareer Apply for Chrome (Free)

    6. JobSeer

    The Chrome extension JobSeer helps filter the right jobs for you. You need to upload your resume, and the JobSeer AI-based algorithm will analyze it based on your skills, qualifications, experience, and more.

    Fill in the relevant job title, location, skills, experience level, and job type, and hit Search Job when you're looking for open positions on job sites. The JobSeer will find a lot of jobs that are right for you.

    Furthermore, it shows you a smartly calculated score on how well you are suited for a specific job and what skills you lack that could hurt your chances. From there, you can decide how to align your skills with specific job descriptions. In addition, JobSeer provides you with five free email addresses of your employers if they have included one on their job listings.

    Using this process, you can filter the best jobs for you, align your resume with job descriptions, email your recruiter to get a handy reference, and all of this in a short time. You should try JobSeer since it's free and will help you level up your job search skills while saving a lot of time.

    Download: JobSeer for Chrome (Free)

    Level Up Your Job Search Process

    Use the tools in the list to simplify your job search. They will make you more efficient and increase your chances of landing your dream job.
