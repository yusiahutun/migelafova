    Table of Biography

    Eric Nenninger is a popular actor from the United States. Eric Nenninger is best known for playing Scott Braddock in the 2003 horror film, “Jeepers Creepers II.”

    Early Life

    Eric Nenninger opened his eyes to the world on the 19th of November 1978, in St. Louis, Missouri, the United States of America. He is currently 45 years old and has a birth sign of Scorpio.

    In addition, he holds American nationality and his birth name is Eric John Nenninger. He practices the Christian religion and he belongs to the White-American ethnicity.

    Meanwhile, he was born to his parents “Joe” Nenninger (father) and Joan Wrobel (mother). Meanwhile, he had a sister whose name was Sheri Nenninger who was already deceased.

    Education

    In terms of his academic qualification, he attended Ladue Horton Watkins High School. He relocated to Los Angeles in 1997 to attend the American Academy of Dramatic Arts. Moreover, he began training at the British American Drama Academy in Oxford in 2000. Following that, he enrolled in The 2nd City Improv School in Chicago and was also trained at Lesly Kahn & Co. in Los Angeles, California.

    Professional Career

    Moving on to his professional career, Eric Nenninger began his acting career in the early 2000s and quickly started gaining recognition for his talent. He made his debut as an actor in the 2000 science fiction television drama series “The X-Files.”

    One of his notable early roles was in the popular teen comedy film “Jeepers Creepers” (2001), where he played the character Scotty. Later, he again reprised the same role in the movie sequel “Jeepers Creepers 2” (2003). He also appeared in other films such as “Reconciliation” (2009), and television films “Mission Control” (2014), “Pharmacy Road” (2017), and “Amy’s Brother” (2017).  Besides working in big-screen movies, he has also played several roles on various television shows.

    Caption: The poster of the 2003 horror film, Jeepers Creepers II (Source: Pinterest)

    Nenninger is perhaps best known for his portrayal of Cadet Eric Hanson in the critically acclaimed HBO miniseries “Malcolm in the Middle” (2000-2002). His performance garnered positive reviews and showcased his ability to bring complex characters to life. Similarly, he portrayed the role of Jesse Overton & officer Collins in the television series “CSI: Crime Scene Investigation” (2000-2009).

    He did his recurring role of Damon Smythe in the comedy-drama series “Glory Daze” appearing in 9 episodes (2010-2011). Meanwhile, his other recurring role in the television series are “Weeds” (2011-2012), “Kickin’ It” (2012-2013), “Wet Hot American Summer: First Day of Camp” (2015), “One Day at a Time” (2017-2019) and others.

    Some of his notable television works are “Generation Kill” (2008), “NCIS” (2008), “24” (2009), “Criminal Minds” (2016), “Living Biblically” (2018), “Black-ish” (2019), “Medical Police” (2020), “American Horror Story: Double Feature” (2021) and “Saturdays” (2023).

    In addition, he made his screenwriting debut in 2019 with the television film “SUBmissions.” Aside from movies and television shows, he has lent his voice to the character Matthew Ryan in the 2011 action-adventure video game “L.A. Noire.”
