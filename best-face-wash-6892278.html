    Our Testing Process

    In order to find the best face wash, we began our process by researching top-rated face cleansers on the market. After identifying 38 promising formulas, we spent 30 hours putting them to the test. A number of volunteers with different skin types and concerns were given a set of face washes and instructions on how to use them. Each person carefully analyzed the performance of the face wash, making notes on cleansing capabilities, hydrating qualities, and overall experience. They rated each product on a scale of 1 to 5 for every category. Once the data was collected, we honed in on the 17 best face washes of 2022.

    What to Keep in Mind

    Skin Type

    Skin type refers to whether your face is oily, dry, normal, combination, or sensitive. The first three are quite self explanatory – oily skin is when your sebaceous glands in the skin make too much sebum. This can cause your pores to clog and feel generally uncomfortable. On the plus side, your skin is perpetually hydrated (which we love). Those with oily skin should look for a strong face wash that can melt every pore-clogging substance away and keep those oils from over producing throughout the day. “A foaming cleanser is more suitable for oily skin to help remove sebum and debris,” says board-certified dermatologist Jenny Liu, M.D. We recommend trying PanOxyl Acne Foaming Wash with Benzoyl Peroxide.

    Dry skin, on the other hand, doesn’t produce as much sebum. This can cause discomfort and leave your skin feeling dehydrated. To keep dry skin soft and moisturized, look for a hydrating face wash. Paula's Choice Optimal Results Hydrating Cleanser, Dr. Loretta Gentle Hydrating Cleanser, and Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser are all great options. “A non-foaming, creamy cleanser is more ideal for dry skin,” explains Dr. Liu.

    Combination skin is exactly what it sounds like: A combination of dry and oily. Gel cleansers, like KraveBeauty Matcha Hemp Hydrating Cleanser can help balance out the inconsistencies, to keep your skin feeling comfortable. 

    Sensitive skin refers to people whose skin easily reacts to external forces. Soaps, scents, detergents, and perfumes can be irritating to such skin, and it requires a very gentle cleanser with minimal ingredients. One of the best picks for sensitive skin is Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser.

    Skin Concerns

    When it comes to skin concerns, one size most certainly does not fit all. Each one — whether it be hyperpigmentation, fine lines, or acne — requires specific attention and ingredients for proper treatment. Where one ingredient might help one concern, it could exasperate another.

    When it comes to fine lines, board-certified dermatologist and founder of Mitchell Dermatology, Hope Mitchell, M.D., advises selecting a face wash that contains hyaluronic acid, retinol, vitamin C, or glycolic acid. All of these ingredients work to minimize the appearance of fine lines in different ways (for example: Hyaluronic acid plumps the skin to fill in wrinkles, while retinol speeds up the cell renewal process). CeraVe Hydrating Facial Cleanser (which contains hyaluronic acid) and Dr. Dennis Gross Skincare Alpha Beta AHA/BHA Daily Cleansing Gel (which is made with glycolic acid) are excellent options to turn to for this reason. On the other hand, if acne is your main concern, then Dr. Mitchell recommends incorporating products with salicylic acid, glycolic acid, or benzoyl peroxide to gently exfoliate and deeply cleanse any acne-causing bacteria. PanOxyl Acne Foaming Wash with Benzoyl Peroxide and Differin Daily Refreshing Cleanser are powerful cleansers that can help fight those breakouts.

    Your Questions, Answered

    How do I wash my face? 

    In order to maximize the benefits of your face wash, you’re going to want to know how to wash your face properly. First, Dr. Liu recommends using lukewarm water — nothing too hot or too cold. Then, “gently massage the cleanser onto your face for 15 to 30 seconds, and rinse off. There’s no need for tools,” she says. Pat, don’t rub, your face dry to finish.

    Some general tips and tricks: Avoid scrubbing too harshly as it will irritate your skin; cold water will close your pores, which prevents the cleanser from doing a proper job; and hot water, on the other hand, will open them too wide, which can make the cleanser irritate your skin by penetrating too deep. 

    How often should I wash my face?

    The short answer is there’s no exact rule book for how often everyone should wash their face because a lot of factors play into it. It can depend on your skin type, the time of day, and your activity level. 

    Dr. Liu says that for most people, washing your face only once or twice a day should suffice. If you opt for once a day, she says it’s “most important to cleanse well before sleep,” to rinse away the dirt and grime from the day. Board-certified dermatologist, Kavita Mariwalla, M.D., advises that those with oily skin should wash their face a minimum of two times a day — in the morning and night — as the natural oils can build up on the skin and ultimately lead to clogged pores and breakouts.

    “People who have oilier skin may discover that they need to wash their face more frequently than those who have dry and sensitive skin,” says Dr. Mitchell. Anyone with normal to dry skin can actually cause damage by washing their face too much. “Excessive washing can cause the skin barrier to be compromised, resulting in red, itchy, dry, or irritated skin,” warns Dr. Mitchell.

    Also, take into consideration whether or not you are going to the gym and engaging in any physical activities. “If you are working out and sweating, you should wash your face afterward,” says Dr. Mariwalla. Sweat from working out can also clog pores, so it’s better to take a quick rinse to prevent that from happening. 

    What are the 3 types of face wash?

    Typically, cleansers either have a foam, gel, or cream texture. Each serves a different purpose and some are better for certain skin types than others. Foaming cleansers are great at removing excess oil. These aren’t as hydrating as others, but provide a deeper clean ideal for those with oily skin. 

    Gel cleansers aren’t as deep cleaning as foaming washes, but that doesn’t mean they’re less efficient — they simply contain more hydrating ingredients and are less likely to strip skin of essential oils. Dr. Mariwalla often recommends gel cleansers for those with oily or acne-prone skin, but they’re gentle enough for those with normal to dry skin, too. Finally, cream cleaners have a thicker consistency and are often extremely moisturizing. This type of cleanser is ideal for those with dry skin.
