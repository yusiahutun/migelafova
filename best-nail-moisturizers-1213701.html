    Like skin, your nails also require a dose of nourishment and hydration. If they fall short of proteins and moisture, then you have to deal with issues like dry cuticles, thin, and brittle nails, etc. So, to prevent any type of nail damage, we have brought to you the best nail moisturizers. These moisturizers not only take good care of your nail plate, nail bed, and primarily cuticles but also promote healthy growth.

    The water and lipid content of your nails is low, as compared to your skin’s stratum corneum. However, nails are extremely permeable to water. When the water content of your nails increases, they become opaque and soft. On the contrary, when there is a decrease in the water content, nails tend to become brittle and dry. Hence, adequate and proper nail moisturization is vital to prevent your nails from becoming brittle (1). Dermatologists at the American Academy of Dermatology Association recommend applying a moisturizing nail care product like petroleum jelly to the cuticles and nails multiple times a day. This practice minimizes the brittleness of your nails and aids in preventing your nails from chipping.

    Speaking of the impact of a nail moisturizer, our contributor Anna Parvatova, Creative Director for SNS Nails, says, “Our cuticles are very delicate and prone to damage. Plus, our nails are naturally dry and brittle due to their low oil and moisture content. Using a nail moisturizer on these areas multiple times per day can help keep such dry areas hydrated, healthy, and nourished. Well-hydrated cuticles and nails can promote nail growth too, so a moisturizer can be really beneficial in the long run.”

    How to Choose Nail Moisturizers?

    Here are a few aspects to consider while splurging on toe or fingernail moisturizers.

    Ingredients: Always ensure that you pick a cuticle oil, moisturizer, or nail cream for damaged nails that is rich in vitamins, minerals, and proteins. Ingredients like vitamin E, jojoba, argan, sunflower, grapeseed, avocado, castor, and olive oils tend to nourish your nails and cuticles thereby promoting healthy nails. You may also invest in moisturizers enriched with aloe vera and beeswax. 

    Type of Moisturizer: There is a wide range of nail moisturizers that help you hydrate your fingernails and toenails to the maximum. What only differs is their formula, texture, and consistency. They are found in the form of oil, gel, cream, lotion, or polishes that keep your nails moisturized and healthy. All these types of nail moisturizers are effective and deliver what they claim. 

    Purpose: Moisturizing the nails isn’t a difficult task if you know what kind of product you need to vouch for. You should always know the purpose of buying one. Some buy a nail moisturizer for hydration and moisturization purposes, whereas others invest in one to stimulate nail growth and heal them completely. For all those who wish to repair nail damage by strengthening the cuticles, then make sure your moisturizer contains the ingredients mentioned above. Avoid purchasing a product just for trial or to join the bandwagon. 

    Formula: Make sure you avoid purchasing products infused with sulfates, parabens, phthalates, and harmful chemicals. The best moisturizer for nails will be free from toxins and contain only natural and organic formula.

    FAQ's

    How can I moisturize my nails naturally?

    You can apply coconut, almond, avocado, olive, or jojoba oil to moisturize your nails naturally. You can also apply petroleum jelly to your cuticles.

    What is the best remedy for dry nails?

    Limiting manicures, avoiding alcohol-based sanitizers, and frequent application of moisturizer or cuticle oil are a few of the best remedies for dry nails.

    What oil is best for dry nails?

    Jojoba oil and vitamin E are the two best oils for nourishing dry nails.
