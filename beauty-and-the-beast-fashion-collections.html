    Disney has been busy with its remakes of late – there has been a new adaptation of Cinderella starring Lily James, a live-action version of The Jungle Book, then the Sleeping Beauty spin-off, Maleficent, starring a pouting Angelina Jolie. But none have managed to inspire the fashion world as much as the upcoming Beauty and the Beast, which arrives in cinemas this weekend.

    The film has reached both the echelons of high fashion – Christopher Kane has teamed up with Disney on a dedicated range – and the high street by way of Juicy Couture and MinkPink. Jewellery labels have also jumped onboard; both Alex Monroe and Swarovski have launched collections inspired by the film's characters, from Lumière to Belle.

    A look from Christopher Kane's Beauty and the Beast collection

    At first glance, perhaps it is to do with Emma Watson playing the title role of Belle – an actress who wears princess gowns well and who has pledged to bring a feminist modernity to the character. But there may be more to it than that.

    So what's the fuss about? Christopher Kane – whose luxury collection of tulle skirts, rose-print sweaters and sheer blouses will speak to mature women, not just Disney aficionados – says the popularity lies within Belle's nonconformist persona. "She's always been an outsider with a strong sense of self, which is why I love her and her spirit," he told us. "She would never conform and she's an individual – she can wear what she wants when she wants. She is the Christopher Kane woman, from humble to extravagant."

    It's true that, compared to the majority of Disney princesses, Belle is more ballsy than most. She refuses to marry the genetically gifted narcissist Gaston (a big deal given that it was first written in the 18th century); she knows who she is, she loves good literature and refuses to conform to society's expectations of her. "There must be more than this provincial life," she sings before being kidnapped by an antisocial, aggressive beast who she then falls in love with, prompting accusations that she is a victim of Stockholm Syndrome – an argument Watson has batted off.

    A look from Christopher Kane's Beauty and the Beast collection

    Nonetheless, she still makes a modern muse for the industry. With Watson's endorsement, this Belle is set to be a more enlightened, independent heroine than Disney has seen before, one even to rival the beloved Elsa from Frozen. Feminism has never been more fashionable; the film has come along at a good time indeed. "Belle is a young woman who isn't afraid to go against the crowd, and a woman who is the master of her own happily-ever-after," said the MinkPink designer Rachel Evans. "We felt as though this would be a perfect match for the MinkPink girl, someone who is passionate, individual and always ahead of the trends."

    MinkPink's line has used the Beauty and the Beast theme loosely; like Christopher Kane, there are no dramatic yellow gowns. This is a range targeted at a 20-something customer: think an off-the-shoulder, ruffled red dress covered in a rose print, a shearling denim jacket with the beast's angry face embroidered on the back and T-shirts featuring slogans associated with the film. The blue pinafore that Belle wears in the first part of the film has been reinvented in the form of navy overalls and her iconic yellow ball gown has become a velvet mustard slip dress. Prices are between £35 and £90. "We studied the finer details and classic quotes, and melded them with current trends and modern slang," explained Evans.

    Minkpink's Beauty and the Beast collection

    "It was our dream to capture the nostalgia of the timeless story with a MinkPink edge; fresh, trend-driven designs that will appeal to both fashion and Disney lovers around the world."

    There are several very visual symbols and looks that make Beauty and the Beast ripe game for the fashion world. Take the enchanted rose for example, which served as Atelier Swarovski's key inspiration for its new range of fairy-tale chokers, rings and bracelets. "Our crystals are a natural partner for the magic of Disney," said Nadja Swarovski, a member of the Swarovski executive board. "But it is also a celebration of the empowering message of strength of heart and soul which is at the centre of the film."

    Swarovski's enchanted rose collection

    Swarovski has also been working with Central Saint Martins students on a collection inspired by the the movie. "As an adult rewatching the film, I found new meanings in the story such as acceptance and inner beauty that inspired my design process," said the CSM BA performance design and practices student Tasmin Case, who created a gloriously grungy look for a contemporary Belle. "It's interesting to work with inner beauty in mind when you create for the exterior. This was illustrated in my design by creating the large beast like shape at the back of the coat, contrasted with the beauty of the embroidery and Swarovski crystals."

    There is also something unique about Beauty and the Beast in its variety of endearing protagonists; the new adaptation will feature Disney's first gay character in Lumière. Outside of Belle and the Beast, the film boasts a cast of wholly loveable personalities – Mrs Potts, Chip, Maurice and Cogsworth.

    A jacket from Minkpink's themed collection

    As Kane said, this is a story of the isolated bound together (something that could be said for many fashion designers, many of whom are famously introverted or who struggled to fit in growing up). The music is unarguably excellent – we all know the tune to 'Be Our Guest'. And visually, it's quite the spectacle. It taps into our childhood memories and fashion loves nothing more than a hint of nostalgia or history reinvented for a 21st-century audience. It's the basis of so much of what it does – just look at the recent Juicy Couture velour-tracksuit revival or the current love of Nineties logos. It's a magic formula of fairy-tale romance, nostalgia with the nonconformist appeal of the outsider thrown in for good measure.

    A shoe from Christopher Kane's Beauty and the Beast collection

    "Growing up I always loved Disney and would only watch Disney films as a child," Kane said. "I love the colours, the characters – it's simply the best. Each of my favourite Disney films holds a fine and special memory for me and they're always on when I visit my niece who is a huge admirer of the films and characters; she always has a Disney princess dress on."

    As we said, it really is a tale as old as time.
