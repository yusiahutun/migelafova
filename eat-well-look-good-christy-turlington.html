    27 Feb 2024, 00:00

    Picture courtesy of Imedeen

    The model reveals her love for sweet potato, avocado and yoga.

    Food philosophy: ‘I’m a big believer in moderation. I’m not one to binge eat or starve myself; I never have been. I do a juice cleanse once a year, and have done since I was in my early twenties. When it’s hot, I want to eat cleaner and opt for raw foods. Generally, to be in a physically and mentally healthy place you should listen to your cravings and try to understand what your body needs. I've always believed in beauty from within and now, in addition to a healthy diet, I boost it by taking supplements, such as Imedeen.’

    Daily diet: ‘I tend to be more of a vegetarian than a carnivore. When I was younger I got into yoga seriously and gave up meat, but then when I fell pregnant I had cravings for protein, so I listened to my body - in the way that yoga teaches you to - and started to eat meat from time to time. I still have a very balanced diet and tend to have a few days as a vegetarian in between occasionally adding a bit of meat and poultry to my meals. It is especially important to me to know where it has come from and if it’s grass-fed or organic.’
    Indulgences: ‘I like more savoury foods; I’ve never really had a sweet tooth. Sea salt is my big indulgence, which is not good for every diet, but I have low blood pressure, so I’m actually encouraged to eat it. I tend to have sea salt, olive oil and lemon on any vegetables that I eat. Avocado is the food that I would eat all year long. My first pregnancy was over the summer and I couldn’t get enough lemon and avocado. Sweet potato is another favourite. When I was pregnant for the second time, with my son, it was all about sweet potatoes. And I think there’s something in that; obviously there’s a certain vitamin (I think A) that I was craving and needing. One of my favourite meals is still sweet potato with tahini.’

    Eating out: 'When I’m in the office, I often order the sweet potato with tahini from Tktktk, in New York, with a double portion of the tahini dressing. If I’m on holiday somewhere tropical I always go for things with guacamole.'

    Wellbeing: ‘Traditionally I haven’t been a big supplement person. I took omegas at some point, but I usually get enough through the food I eat, so I stopped. Now I take Imedeen Time Perfection because it contains ingredients that I can’t naturally find in my diet, like grapeseed extract, which protects the collagen in your skin. It’s unique in the way that it is specific to skin regeneration. I take it every morning after I brush my teeth. I started taking it six months ago and my skin, which has gotten dryer over the years, feels so much better. If I feel like I’m coming down with something I’ll add some probiotic powder to a smoothie.’

    Workout: ‘I do yoga a couple of times a week, which I absolutely love. I go to Kula, a studio in Tribeca, which is up four flights of stairs, so you get your little cardio workout running up to class. Yoga is so much more than just physical activity. I meditate at the end, so I get to relax, and it’s also like having a massage, but a self-massage.’
