    Mario Batali is an American personality who is a chef by profession. Despite this, he is a writer and a founder of many restaurants who is managing all the responsibilities single-handedly. He is a television personality who had his consistent appearance on various Food Network shows and channels being featured on Molto Mario and Iron Chef America. In 2017, the restaurant site reviews revealed that he was involved in various sexual misconduct because of which he had to sell his restaurant holding and had to step back from various television food shows.

    Early Life

    Mario Batali was born on 19th September 1960 in Seattle, Washington to his parents named Marilyn (LaFramboise) and Armandino Batali, who together founded Salumi restaurant in Seattle in 2006. Batali’s father is of Italian descent and his mother belongs of French-Canadian ancestry. Batali went to Rutgers University in New Brunswick, New Jersey, where he developed himself into a chef while cooking at various restaurants/pubs.

    Mario Batali Career

    At the age of 29, Batali became sous chef at the Four Seasons Biltmore after he began working for the Four Seasons Clift Hotel San Francisco. Batali has also worked for the San Francisco restaurant, Stars with the chef Jeremiah Tower. Batali also appeared in the famous and most-watched show on the Food Network called Molto Mario, which aired from 1996 to 2004. The show became popular and Batali soon became a household name while getting popularized with the Food Network. In 1998, along with other founders and contributors, Batali formed the B&B Hospitality Group, which came to be known as Batali & Bastianich Hospitality Group in the later years. In 2017, Batali became the co-host of the ABC daytime talk show called The Chew which had its premiere in 2011 and was aired until 2017.
