    The 5 Best Social Networking Platforms for Book Lovers

    If you love to talk about books, there are social media platforms dedicated to book lovers who love to discuss their favorite reads. The platforms are designed to give you recommendations, keep you up to date with new releases, and allow you to leave reviews.

    You can also form your own book clubs on some. Let's take a look at where you can find books and connect with book lovers on the web.

    1. Goodreads

    If you're looking for some of the best book review sites, Goodreads is one of them. There's so much you can do on the site that it might be difficult to know where to start. For example, you can see what your friends are reading, but at the same time, you can track your own reading activities. You can add books you've read and books you want to read to your personal profile as well.

    You'll get personalized book recommendations based on your interests. You can leave reviews, follow people (even authors themselves), and see which books are trending right now. The platform allows you to add friends, receive messages, join and follow discussions, as well as browse your favorite genres.

    You'll feel like a part of a very large community when you vote for your favorite books and discover readers' most anticipated books of the year. Set your own reading challenges and track your progress to see if you can beat your targets. Goodreads is one of those communities you won't get bored being a part of.

    2. Litsy

    Close

    Litsy has been called the Instagram of the book-loving community. It is a must-have app for book lovers. You can post pictures on your feed and people can like, comment, and share posts. You can easily search for books and leave reviews. You'll get recommended books based on your interests as well. Litsy has a thriving community where people gather to talk about books.

    You can mark books you are currently reading, have read, or want to read. The rating system ranges from "Pick," "So-So," "Pan," and "Bail" which shows corresponding icons to help you decide how you feel about the book you are reviewing.

    Just like other social media sites, you can scroll through your feed to find what interests you, then interact with the post. You can follow users and see their feeds, as well as search their profiles for books they have recommended.

    3. LibraryThing

    LibraryThing is a virtual library that allows you to catalog the books you own, have read, or want to read. Just like other book networking platforms, you can rate, leave reviews, and aggregate tags. You'll also find information about your favorite authors.

    The more you contribute to the site, the better your recommendations will be, but you'll also improve the catalog for others. You can connect with people based on the books you both like as well.

    4. TikTok (BookTok)

    Close

    If you haven't heard of BookTok, it's time to get into a huge sub-community that emerged on the TikTok app. Creators make videos, review books, discuss literature, and grow their TikTok followers by posting stuff about the books they are reading. The community is also known as "BookTokers" and the genres that they focus on range from young adult fiction, fantasy, and romance.

    You'll also find genres like BIPOC and LGBTQ+. Some BookTok videos have gone viral, and this is why the community gained a huge following. When people recommend books through the BookTok community, some books experience significant sales. Many have made it to bestseller lists.

    Just like anything on TikTok, you can comment on the book recommendation, follow the creator, share, save, as well as like people's videos.

    5. Reddit

    Reddit is an excellent platform for any type of community. There are subreddits with millions of members who are ready and willing to discuss and recommend books. It's easy to look for what you are interested in. For example, if your favorite books are fantasy sagas, all you need to do is look for a subreddit that is dedicated to books of that genre.

    You can join as many subreddits as you like, follow discussions, comment, share posts, and much more. Some Reddit threads have weekly recommendations, a list of books people are reading, and book clubs. No matter what you're into, there is a chance that there is a subreddit dedicated to it on the Reddit platform.

    Make Friends With Book Lovers

    Social networks are designed to help people interact and engage with one another. If you're a book-lover who is excited about sharing knowledge and participating in live discussions about books you are currently reading, some of these platforms may be perfect for your needs. Reading is certainly not a lonely activity.
