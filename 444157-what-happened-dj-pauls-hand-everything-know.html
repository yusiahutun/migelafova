    DJ Paul's deformed arm

    Several fans have wondered why DJ Paul from 36 Mafia's hand looks deformed. Paul was born with a condition known as Erb's palsy, which is otherwise known as brachial plexus injury.

    The abnormality occurs during difficult childbirth when the child's neck is stretched in a way that damages the brachial plexus, a network of nerves running down the spine and gives feeling to the nerves in the arms, hands and wrists.

    DJ Paul's Erbs Palsy has caused his right hand, which was affected, to grow more slowly than the other arm. Brachial plexus injury occurs in one out of 1000 children and can be temporary or permanent. The disc jockey likes to jokingly call his right hand his ''baby arm.''

    His fans can see DJ Paul's arm without a cast despite his condition. The award-winning hitmaker loves to spin using his left hand, which has seen him earn the respect of colleagues in the industry and fans of his work.
