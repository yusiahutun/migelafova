    Here's what you need to know about music icon Richard Marx. 

    Who is Richard Marx?

    Richard Marx is a musician, songwriter and record producer who was born on September 16, 1963. He was born in Chicago, Illinois.

    Richard began his career at five years old, singing commercial jingles. His big break came at 17 when Lionel Richie got a hold of a tape of his songs and urged him to come to Los Angeles, California.

    Richard's debut album came out in 1987. It was self-titled and included four hit singles, like Don't Mean Nothing, Should've Known Better and Endless Summer Nights.

    The album was successful and allowed Richard to set out on his very first world tour on which he opened for REO Speedwagon. 

    He has since released 10 more studio albums and a single Grammy Award.

    Richard has been married twice. He and Cynthia Rhodes tied the knot in 1989 and stayed married until 2014. The following year he married Daisy Fuentes, whom he is still married to.

    Who is Daisy Fuentes?

    Daisy Fuentes is Richard Marx's wife. She's known for being the first Latina VJ on MTV and the first Latina spokesperson for Revlon.

    She was born on November 17, 1966 in Havana, Cuba. Her family left there when she was about three and moved to Madrid, Spain and, eventually, Harrison, New Jersey. 

    Daisy graduated in 1984 and dreamed of being a hairstylist. She attended cosmetology school only to wind up enrolling at Bergen Community College where she studied communications.

    She was hired to present the weather on WNJU-TV 47, a local Telemundo affiliate. Not long after, she joined a Univision affiliate called WXTV-TV 41 where she presented weather and evening news. 

    In 1988 Daisy was asked to host MTV Internacional. She joined MTV in 1993, but continued to host shows on MTV Latin America. She was also hosting House of Style at the time. 

    Daisy has appeared in ads for companies like Revlon, Pantene, American Express, M&Ms and more.
