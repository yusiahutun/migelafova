    Jean Todt is a French motorsport executive who was born on February 25, 1946. He gained a reputation in motor sport administration after a successful career as a rally co-driver, first with Peugeot Talbot Sport, then with Scuderia Ferrari, before being named Chief Executive Officer of Ferrari from 2004 to 2008. He has been President of the Fédération Internationale de l’Automobile since October 23, 2009. (FIA). Peugeot won four World Rally Championship championships (drivers and manufacturers) under his leadership, won the Paris-Dakar Rally four times, and won the Le Mans 24 Hours twice. During his tenure at Ferrari, the Scuderia won 14 World Championships in Formula One (drivers and manufacturers).
