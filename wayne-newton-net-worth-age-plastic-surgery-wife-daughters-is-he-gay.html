    Wayne Newton Bio, Age

    Wayne Newton may have been born in Norfolk Virginia but his fate was in the city of Sin. Born of an auto mechanic and his wife Evelyn Marie Smith Wayne is a man of Native American origin who grew up in a modest family. He became interested in music at an early age learning musical instruments at the age of six.

    When his family moved to Ohio Newark to be exact Wayne began what would become his career singing in entertainment venues. Wayne with his brother Jerry started singing in local theatres and clubs.

    Although Newton fought asthma as a child he fought it and took an important step early in his career performing before Dwight D. Eisenhower a former US president.

    After finishing high school he and his brother got their first show through a Las Vegas booking agent who saw one of their shows on the Lew King Rangers Show and booked the brothers to play for two weeks in Las Vegas. A two-week contract turned into a five-year contract because the brothers were loved by the beloved Las Vegas crowd.

    Newton obtained his first professional recording contract in 1963 after signing with Capitol Records. He released his first album reaching 13th place on the Billboard Hot 100.

    In music, industry support is crucial to success and fortunately for Newton, it has not been lacking. He received significant support from Lucile Ball Danny Thomas Bobby Darin and some other important entertainment icons at the time. This support has helped to advance Newton’s career to the point where he has been ranked number one in Canada with his song Daddy Don’t You Walk So Fast.

    In 1994 Newton became a popular name in music and especially in Vegas. He presented his 25,000th solo exhibition in Vegas in 1994. He signed a 10-year contract with Stardust a Vegas casino to be a regular artist. A similar agreement was also signed with the Hilton.

    After years of headlining shows and entertaining guests in various halls in Vegas Newton was elected to the Gaming Hall of Fame in 2000. Newton also got into reality TV by hosting a show on E! called The Entertainer. He still performs in Vegas in a show called Up Close & Personal.

    His career has been marked by many awards and distinctions. In addition to being elected to the Gaming Hall of Fame Newton was also President of the USO Celebrity Circle. He received the Woodrow Wilson Award for Public Service.

    Music has been an important part of Newton’s career but he is also a horse lover. He has a ranch dedicated to the breeding of Arabian horses and has been able to produce more than 700 foals. His service for the art of breeding horses especially Arabian horses was recognized in 2007 when he received a prize from the Association of Arabian Horse Breeders for his achievements.

    Solo Success

    In 1962 Bobby Darin provided support for Wayne in launching his solo career. This yielded great success with “Danke Schoen” entering the Top 20 charts the following year. 1965 saw an even greater success with “Red Roses for a Blue Lady” hitting the charts and becoming one of Newton’s signature songs. His last major single was released in 1972 titled “Daddy Don’t You Walk So Fast” which featured his trademark pencil-thin mustache and jazzed-up on-stage look along with a shift to a lower vocal range.

    At the height of his career, Newton was one of the most successful entertainers in Las Vegas and beyond. He graced television variety shows with guest appearances and held a residency at casinos around Nevada’s famous city. During this time he was also known as the highest-paid act in Vegas. Today Newton continues to perform concerts across America while supporting U.S. troops through USO tours that bring entertainment to military personnel stationed overseas.

    Is Wayne Newton Gay? Wife and Daughter

    The life of an artist especially that of a popular artist like Wayne is not exactly ideal to have a family. The often busy schedules make it difficult to spend enough time with family members but Wayne is trying to make it work. However, he has not always been successful in his attempts as evidenced by his divorce from his first wife Elaine Okamura. The couple married in 1968 but finally divorced in 1985. They share a child Erin Newton.

    In 1994 Newton married again this time Kathleen McCrone. Newton made more effort to make the relationship work because it is known that he takes long breaks to spend time with his family. Newton and Kathleen have a daughter together, Lauren Ashley Newton.

    Despite Johnny Carson’s jokes about Newton’s sexuality suggesting that the artist is gay Newton is a straight man.
