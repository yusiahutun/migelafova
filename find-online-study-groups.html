    Studying for tests and exams isn’t the most fun thing to do, and sometimes, you can get demotivated. Fortunately, study groups are an effective way to ensure you allocate time for your studies. Not only that, but you get to experience being part of a team, allowing you to improve in your studies.

    If you’re someone who studies remotely, or finds it hard to join physical study groups, you might feel like you have no option but to study alone. But, that doesn’t have to be the case. Let’s take a look at five websites to find online study groups.

    1. Capegoat

    Capegoat is a website that has an online community of global students who study together and support each other's goals. Capegoat has over 5,000 students, with over 100,000 hours spent studying.

    When you study with Capegoat, you can either join an existing virtual study room, or you can create your own. Capegoat helps you stay productive by allowing you to track your progress. You can access data to track the time you’ve spent, your completed tasks, and much more.

    If you need a little push to stay productive, this platform allows you to set timers, create lists, and provides opportunities for you to keep track of your accomplishments.

    2. Study Together

    If you want to meet and chat with students from all over the world, then this is the platform for you. Study Together hosts one of the largest global student communities, with over one million members, and 19 million study sessions.

    With a variety of features to choose from, you're bound to reach your study goals. Study Together has live study rooms that you can join at any time of the day.

    If you’re not inspired by any of the available study rooms, Study Together allows you to create your own study universe with personalized features.

    3. StudyStream

    StudyStream is a platform that not only helps you join study sessions with other students online, but it aims to help you stay organized and productive. This helps you to have more effective study sessions.

    On StudyStream, you can join focus rooms where you can join other students to study, or you can join other professionals to help you focus on work projects. This platform isn’t just about work and study, but it offers various weekly online events, which include, journaling, wellness sessions, productivity events, and a virtual girls’ room with hour long chats.

    To access all virtual rooms on StudyStream, you simply have to sign up.

    4. Studyverse

    If you’re looking for ways to avoid distraction and procrastination, this is the perfect website for you. Studyverse aims to use social accountability to help you thrive in your studies.

    You can invite your friends to join your study room, or you can join an existing public room. Each study room has a unique theme, group timers, and music to set the mood.

    On Studyverse, you can view your statistics to track your study time, and you can check how many study rooms you’ve joined.

    5. Fiveable

    Fiveable serves as a virtual library with study tools and friends to study with. With over ten million study guide users and 300,000 study sessions, there's no doubt that you'll get the help you need to study successfully.

    On Fiveable, you can access study rooms with timers, video calls, music integration with popular music platforms, and you can choose between solo and multiplayer mode. Fiveable also has a large variety of AP guides for every subject to help you prepare for exams.

    Take Studying to the Next Level

    Whether you want to learn how to focus better while studying, or just want to join a community of other students, these sites could benefit you. And if you’re looking for other ways to enhance the way you study, there are plenty of online tools to boost your studying and help you stay organized.
