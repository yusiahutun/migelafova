    As a nod to its beloved Book Tote, Dior has launched a new series which celebrates reading. The Dior Book Tote Club sees friends of the brand talk through the books which have had the biggest impact on their lives, including British actress Rosamund Pike, who opens up about her love of Joan Didion, and explains why she cannot get on board with speed reading.

    Walking around Hatchards, London's oldest bookshop, after closing time, the actress talks through some of her favourite titles. Her list includes Jack Kerouac's 'On The Road', which she read as a teenager, Joan Didion's book of essays, 'Slouching Towards Bethlehem' ("She is one of the most concise and pertinent observers of people," Pike says) and Hanya Yanagihara's 'A Little Life'.

    CHRISTIAN DIOR

    "This is truly one of the most brilliant, devastating, magnificent novels I have ever read," she says of 'A Little Life'. "It's a book that will make you cry. I don't know how she wrote this book. It's one of these books that makes you think, 'How did this come out of you?' It's a book I didn't want to leave behind."

    CHRISTIAN DIOR

    Pike also selects Barnaby Barford's photobook 'The Apple Is Everything' due to her love of the fruit, and opens up about how she has recently discovered graphic novels, while adding that she will always turn to Thomas Hardy when she's away from home and missing England.

    "Books move you but it's also really wonderful when books make you laugh," she adds. "And the wonderful thing about being in a bookshop is that you can travel the world while sitting still."
