    Early Life and Education

    The renowned actress Shannon Woodward was born on December 17, 1984, in Phoenix, Arizona. She moved with her family to Florida when she was a child and attended Olympic Heights Community High School in Boca Raton.

    When she was studying at her school, she began her acting there which impressed the casting director of Nickelodeon’s “Clarissa Explains It All.” When Woodward turned 18, she moved to LA where she met her best friend Katy Perry, and she also starred in several local community plays.

    Career

    One of the promising actresses in Hollywood film industry, Shannon first appeared onscreen and came into limelight, in 1991 when she played the role of Missy on Nickelodeons. During the show’s five seasons she reprises the role twice. 

    She also appeared alongside Clarissa star Melissa Joan Hart in a TV drama “Family Reunion: A Relative Nightmare.”

    Woodward then appeared in the role of Lucy in her second movie “Tornado.” She also played an uncredited role in the CBS miniseries entitled “True Women.”

    After playing minor roles on the small screen, she got an opportunity to act in a big series entitled “The Riches" which aired for two seasons on cable network FX. After the end of The Riches, she landed a role of Leah in the teen supernatural/horror film “The Haunting of Molly Hartley.”

    Shannon also landed a lead role on FOX's popular sitcom Raising Hope, along side Cloris Leachman and Martha Plimpton. The show started in September 2010 and ended on April 2014.

    Currently, she plays Elsie Hughes, a programmer, as a series regular in the HBO series entitled “Westworld.”
