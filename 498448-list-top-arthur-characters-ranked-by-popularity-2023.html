    Arthur is a PBS Kids television show based on the Arthur book series by Marc Brown. Arthur characters follow the adventures of Arthur Read, an eight-year-old aardvark, and his friends in the fictional town of Elwood City. The show primarily targets children between the ages of four and eight and is known for its educational content, tackling issues such as bullying, peer pressure, and diversity.

    Arthur characters are diverse and include anthropomorphic animals such as rabbits, monkeys, dogs, and humans. Since its premiere in 1996, the show's catchy music and enduring characters have helped to establish it as a cherished children's classic. The show's portrayal of strong, intelligent, and diverse female characters has been praised for providing positive role models for young viewers.

    List of top Arthur characters

    The characters in the animated TV series Arthur are central to the show's popularity and success. Each character has a personality and contributes something unique to the film, which helps to make the cast exciting and diverse. Here is a list of Arthur characters ranked by popularity.

    1. Arthur Read

    Arthur Read is the main character in the Arthur children's television series. He is an eight-year-old aardvark who lives in the fictional town of Elwood City. Arthur encounters some challenges throughout the series, including bullying, peer pressure, and self-doubt, but he always triumphs over them with the support of his family and friends.

    Arthur is a likeable and approachable protagonist who gains valuable life lessons from each episode. He is well-recognised for having a kind heart and being curious and tenacious. However, he has a few close buddies with whom he frequently hangs out. His friends include Francine Frensky and Muffy Crosswire.

    2. Buster Baxter

    Buster Baxter is a character in the Arthur children's television series. He is a curious and daring bunny, one of Arthur Read's closest buddies. Buster is renowned for his ravenous appetite, love of exploration, and propensity to find himself in absurd circumstances.

    3. D.W. Read

    D.W. Read, also known as Dora Winifred, is one of the female Arthur characters. She is the younger sister to the lead character, Arthur Read and the middle child in the family. She appears in the series at the beginning as four years old and gets older as it goes on.

    4. Binky Barnes

    What animal is Binky from Arthur? Binky is a bulldog boy and one of Arthur's close friends. He has a reputation for displaying a tough façade and occasionally acting like a harsh guy, but he also has a soft side and a decent heart.

    Although Binky's real name is Shelley Barnes, he prefers to go by the nickname Binky since he enjoys the Binky comic book series. Fans of the show love the character Binky, who has appeared in many episodes and spin-off content.

    5. Nigel Ratburn

    Nigel Ratburn is an anthropomorphic aardvark known for his no-nonsense approach to teaching and high academic standards. He is also famous for giving his students a lot of homework, projects and tests. His tough teaching style is inspired by his schoolteacher, Mr Pryce-Jones, whom he greatly respects as his best teacher.

    Despite his rigid attitude, he truly cares about his pupils and frequently goes over and above to support them. Since the beginning of the animated Arthur TV series, Ratburn has regularly appeared in the series and has become one of the show's most adored characters.

    6. Francine Alice Frensky

    What animal is Francine from Arthur? Francine is an anthropomorphic monkey with brown hair. She is one of the female Arthur characters and has also appeared in many episodes and the show's spin-off.

    She is depicted as exceptionally bright and academically talented. Francine has difficulties and experiences throughout the series, including figuring out how to deal with losing, sticking up for herself, and navigating friendships.

    7. Alan "The Brain" Powers

    Alan is a brilliant and curious character depicted as having a vast knowledge base and a deep love for learning. He often spends his time reading books and conducting experiments hence having brain powers. He is one of Mr Nigel Ratburn's students and is often considered the most intelligent student in his class.

    Despite his brilliance, Alan occasionally finds it difficult to relate to his classmates and could come across as overly serious or socially awkward. He also demonstrates a lovely heart and genuine concern for his companions.

    Kate is the youngest member of the Read family and is the younger sister of Arthur and D.W. Read. Kate is depicted as a curious and adventurous toddler who is always eager to explore and learn new things.

    Despite her young age, Kate comes out as highly independent and strong-willed, and she is seen exploring her surroundings with passion and curiosity.     12. Fern Walters

    Fern Walters character is depicted as a quiet and thoughtful character who enjoys spending time alone and writing in her journal. She is often seen reading and writing and is known for her writing talent. She is a fellow Lakewood Elementary School student known for her intelligence, writing skills, and passion for environmental causes.

    13. Mrs Sarah MacGrady

    Mrs Sarah MacGrady is one of the significant female Arthur characters in the Arthur children's television series. She is the school librarian at Lakewood Elementary School, where Arthur and his friends attend.

    14. Sue Ellen Armstrong

    Sue Ellen Armstrong is a sporty and intelligent third-grade student at Lakewood Elementary School who moved to Elwood City from the fictional country of The Great Plains.

    Sue Ellen is renowned for her many hobbies and talents, including reading comic books, yoga, and playing the guitar. She is a gifted athlete who excels in sports like basketball and soccer. As a character from a different country, Sue Ellen adds diversity to the show and allows one to explore different cultural experiences.

    15. Bitzi Baxter

    Bitzi Baxter is the mother of Buster Baxter and a news reporter for the Elwood City Newspaper. In the television series, Bitzi is portrayed as Buster's loving mother. She supports his sense of adventure and curiosity and is always available to lend support and counsel.
