    Chairman of the Lagos chapter of the National Union of Road Transport Workers (NURTW), Musiliu Akinsanya aka MC Oluomo, has been hailed for his efforts in preserving the memory of his late mother, Alhaja Sinatu Abebi Akinsanya.

    MC Oluomo built and donated a fully furnished mosque in honour of his deceased parent who passed away in April 2020 at the age of 87.

    A video of the mosque named after the deceased was spotted in the online community and from indications, the NURTW boss dug deep into his pocket to complete the project.

    An interior view of the mosque captured a huge and expensive-looking chandelier hanging from the ceiling.

    The mosque also features a fully-tiled ablution center for congregants and comes with air-conditioning systems.

    In a separate story, Cardinal John Onaiyekan, also the former archbishop of Abuja on Thursday, April 22, showed great love towards Muslims at the Al-Habibiyah Mosque in Guzape.

    According to Channels TV, before his arrival, the faithful had gathered to break their evening fast. The priest handed plates of food to almost 2,000 worshippers while preaching peace.

    He said that God hated people kill each other, stating that the Almighty wanted everybody to live together in peace and harmony.

    On the same occasion, the chief Imam of the mosque, Fuad Adeyemi, said that the clergy's food contributed greatly to their energy.

    “This is not an underground clergy. It shows that we can really live together, that we can be friends, and that we can live in peace,” he said.
