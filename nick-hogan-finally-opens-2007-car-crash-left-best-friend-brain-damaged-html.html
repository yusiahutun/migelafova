    It has been over three years since the crash that almost killed his best friend John Graziano and landed him in jail.

    But Nick Hogan, the 20-year-old son of wrestler Hulk Hogan,has finally broken his silence.

    Speaking publicly for the first time, Nick appeared on ABC's Good Morning America this morning and broke down in tears during the interview as he discussed how the accident has changed his life.

    Come clean: Nick Hogan talks for the first time on GMA this morning about his near fatal car accident that left his friend brain damaged

    On the night of August 26 2007, Nick and his best friend John Graziano were driving in a Yellow Toyota Supra to a restaurant in Clearwater, Florida for dinner.

    Two of their friends were in another of the Hogan family cars, a silver Dodge Viper, and on the way to the restaurant the two cars began to drag race.

    Nick, who was driving the Supra, lost control of the vehicle and it crashed.

    Both Nick and John were airlifted to hospital where Nick was later released but John, who had not been wearing a seatbelt, sustained severe eye and brain injuries and will require 24-hour-a-day care for the rest of his life.

    The former US marine, had recently returned from a tour in Iraq but has now been left in a semi conscious state since the accident.

    Too much: The 20-year-old broke down as he discussed the incident

    Hogan was charged with reckless driving involving serious bodily injury, use of a motor vehicle in commission of a felony, a person under the age of 21 operating a vehicle with a blood alcohol level of 0.02 or higher, and illegal window tint.

    He was sentenced to eight months in Pinellas County Jail as well as five years of probation, 500 community service hours, and his driver's license was suspended for 3 years.

    He ended up serving justunder six months of his sentence and was released early for good behavior.

    It's hard: He claims there are days when he struggles to get out of bed

    'It's hard to move on every day and be positive when there's something that's so horribly devastating,' Nick told GMA.

    'There's days when I can't even get out of bed.'

    He admitted that the incident still haunts him on  daily basis.
