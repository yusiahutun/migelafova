    Girl born with brain growing outside her skull survives

    - A girlwas born with her the growing through a hole in the skull - Doctors gave heronly 25% chance of survival but she managed to survive - She has since reached all her milestones including walking and talking

    26 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - A girl was born with her the growing through a hole in the skull

    - Doctors gave her only 25% chance of survival but she managed to survive

    - She has since reached all her milestones including walking and talking

    Isabella Grote, who is now four years old, was born with part of her brain growing outside her skull.

    Her parents, Sharlotte, 40, and Jim, 49, were told to prepare for the worst as doctors feared she wouldn't survive a day and highly likely to be severely disabled for the rest of her life.

    The devastated parents even started planning the funeral of their baby girl who underwent lifesaving surgery when she was just three days old.

    Despite all negative doctors' prognoses the miracle baby survived all possible diseases and is thriving in Minot, North Dakota, USA.

    Isabella has hit all her milestones including walking and talking -- something her parents could not even dream to see.

    Recalling first days of her baby daughter mother Sharlotte said: "Abortion was never an option for us, even if I could spend just a few minutes with my little girl I would be happy.

    I called a funeral home to find out our options as we prepared ourselves for the worst. On May 4, 2012, doctors delivered Isabella by emergency C-section after she hadn't grown in two weeks. Seeing her for the first time I was terrified, all I could see was this sac on the back of her head.

    Doctors told us that there was less brain matter in her sac than originally thought, which was such a relief. At three days old she went into surgery to have the sac removed but we still didn't know whether she would make it.

    READ ALSO: Meet girl born with giant head who cheated death (photos)

    Luckily the surgery went amazing, there was mostly fluid in the sack and we were informed Isabella had no other obvious abnormalities.

    Doctors were even able to place the small part of her brain back into her skull - we couldn't believe it."

    Shorlty afterwards Sharlotte and her husband Jim Grote were able to take their little girl home.

    They faced an anxious future as they still didn't know whether she would be able to walk or talk. Miraculously Isabella started to make huge progress sooner than expected.

    At around nine months old she was able to sit up by herself and at 18 months she took her first steps.

    Isabella's speech was slow at first but improved massively when she went to pre-school age three and was soon able to talk in full sentences.

    READ ALSO: Meet little boy who gradually turns into stone statue (photos)

    Despite two surgeries at the age of two and three to correct vision problems, Isabella is a picture of health.

    "We feel truly blessed to have our beautiful little girl with us today. She has been through more than most people have to go through in a lifetime.

     She is just like any other four year old, she loves playing with her dolls and is so loving and caring. She is such a fighter," Mrs Grote said.

    Encephalocele is a neural tube defect characterized by sac-like protrusions of the brainand the membranes that cover it through openings in the skull.

    These defects are caused by failure of the neural tube to close completely during fetal development. Its severity varies, depending on its location.
