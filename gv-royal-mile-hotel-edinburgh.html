    The only five-star hotel situated on Edinburgh's iconic Royal Mile, the G&V is a warm and welcoming splash of colour set amidst the grand, grey buildings of Edinburgh's historic Old Town. The hotel is renowned for championing local artistic talents; the result being a bright, eclectic mixture of rooms and communal areas. The more you look, the more there is to see.

    Accommodation

    Thanks to clever design, each of the 136 rooms in the G&V boasts views overlooking the city; after all, that is what visitors are there to see. Clean, elegant and comfortable, each bedroom features a selection of modern touches to make your stay as effortless as possible: including iPod-docking stations, smart TVs and Nespresso machines (plus underfloor heating in the bathroom, which is always a bonus). For something a little different, book into one of the designer suites; each has been decorated from floor to ceiling by a local Scottish artist, giving it its own unique character and charm.

    Food and drink

    The hotel's award-winning Italian restaurant, Cucina, is popular among tourists and locals alike, so you won't just find hotel guests dining there. Featuring a menu of seasonal, modern cooking from the head chef Mattia Camorani and team, tuck into maple-glazed duck on toasted ciabatta or slow-cooked beef cheek with mashed potato, followed by a selection of home-made ice -creams. On warm summer evenings, eat out on the twinkling fairy-lit terrace.

    The hotel's Bar G&V is a lively watering hole which, despite its central location, feels far away from the touristy hustle-and-bustle and non-stop bagpipes. Zesty colours, mirror balls and upbeat music immediately lend a sense of fun, while the merest glance at the cocktail menu is enough to make your mouth water. 

    Location

    The G&V is unrivalled in its location; which is ideal for both returning and first-time visitors, eager to explore everything that Edinburgh has to offer. The only five-star hotel on the Royal Mile, you're a stone's throw away from some of the city's top attractions; at one end of the Mile you'll find the impressive Edinburgh Castle, just a short stroll away, while at the other lies Arthur's Seat, the main peak of a group of hills, from which you'll see the most incredible vistas across the city.
