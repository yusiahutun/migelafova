    She might still be just starting out in Hollywood, but she's already stolen the spotlight on more than one occasion.

    And all eyes were on actress Kathryn Newton at the 26th Annual Screen Actors Guild Awards at the Shrine Auditorium in Los Angeles, California.

    The 22-year-old actress turned heads in a stunning orange Valentino gown.

    Hollywood princess: Kathryn Newton enjoyed the spotlight while on the red carpet of the SAG Awards on Sunday

    On screen family: Kathryn posed with her TV mom Reese Witherspoon at the event

    Newton wowed in a voluminous orange-peach gown, which featured winged shoulders, a low bust which met an empire waist, and ruffles galore on the fanned-out, A-line skirt.

    The Blockers starlet wore her blond hair slicked back in a chic style, and wore simple jeweled earrings as her only accessories.

    Kathryn played outspoken older daughter Abigail to Reese Witherspoon's control freak Madeline in both seasons of HBO's acclaimed series Big Little Lies, which was nominated at the awards show for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Drama Series. 

    After getting her start as a youngster on All My Children in 2002, Kathryn went on to land a recurring role on the Jay Mohr TV show Gary Unmarried from 2008 to 2010. 

    From there it was onward and upwards for Newton, as she secured appearances in Paranormal Activity 4, Mad Men and more recently, award-winning films like Lady Bird and Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri in 2017.

    Around the same time, the young actress also enjoyed repeated appearances in three TV series: Halt and Catch Fire, Supernatural and the miniseries Little Women. 

    Selfie time: Inside the venue, she stopped for a selfie with her BLL mom Reese Witherspoon, who also looked radiant in a tight and bedazzled black and silver gown

    Abigail and Madeline: The 22-year-old actress played outspoken older daughter Abigail to Reese Witherspoon's control freak Madeline in HBO's acclaimed series Big Little Lies

    On the feature film front, Kathryn held her own opposite the incredible Leslie Mann as her mother in the underappreciated raucous comedy Blockers in 2018, and also turned in well-received performances in Pokémon Detective Pikachu and the Julia Roberts drama Ben Is Back.

    Currently, with Big Little Lies wrapped (for now), Kathryn can also be seen on the Netflix series The Society, and will next appear on the silver screen in an 'untitled body swap thriller' costarring Vince Vaughn.

    In that film, she'll play a high school student who unwittingly switches bodies with a deranged serial killer.
