    William Ruto Steps out In Sugoi with Treasured Herding Stick He Was Gifted Over 20 Years Ago

    President William Ruto is in his home village in Sugoi, Uasin Gishu county for the holidays. Ruto and his wife Rachel gifted area residents with goodies for Christmas Day, and he was seen interacting with the crowd.

    12 Aug 2024, 00:00
    • President William Ruto held a meeting with Sugoi residents and treated them to goodies for Christmas
    • The politician was spotted excitedly greeting a group of women while carrying a herding stick he owned for over 20 years
    • Ruto previously disclosed how he acquired the stick while giving a tour of his impressive farm

    President William Ruto is in his home village in Sugoi, Uasin Gishu county for the holidays.

    William Ruto's herding stick

    Ruto and his wife Rachel gifted area residents with goodies for Christmas Day, and he was seen interacting with the crowd.

    One image depicted Ruto greeting a group of seated women while holding a herding stick. Unknown to many, the politician treasures the hoarding stick and has owned it for over two decades.

    Ruto first spoke about the stick in 2012 during a TV interview with K24. The station visited his farm soon after he became deputy president and he showed them around.

    However, before starting the tour, Ruto ordered one of his farm workers to get him the beloved walking stick. He then explained how he had acquired it.

    “Wapi fimbo yangu wewe (where is my herding stick)? Hii fimbo is for herding cattle. I was given by some people after I did something for them. It is about 18 years old,” he said.

    Ten years after the interview, Ruto is the president and still owns the herding stick, making it around 28 years old.

    Inside William Ruto’s Sugoi farm

    During the interview, Ruto showcased the expansive farm where he practises beekeeping, plants maize for food and fodder for the cows and numerous fruits, including mangoes, avocado, and rears bees in around 100 hives.

    “My wife brought 5,000 chickens from India, so we also sell eggs,” he said.

    Aside from the eggs, Ruto’s Sugoi farm also sells improved chicken as it has an industrial-grade incubator that can hatch thousands of chicks monthly.

    Ruto finally revealed how much he made from selling eggs when he fell out with Uhuru, and former Interior CS Fred Matiang’i publicly shared some of the properties he owns.

    "They could have added that in that my poultry business, I have 200,000 chickens and that I get 150,000 eggs daily which earns me KSh1.5 million daily," he said.
