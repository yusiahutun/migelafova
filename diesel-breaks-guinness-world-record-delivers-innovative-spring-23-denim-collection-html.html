    15 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Diesel unveiled its spring/summer 2023 all-gender collection and broke a Guinness World Record with the world’s largest ever inflatable sculpture at their live runway show in Milan, Italy.

    The show, which marked the start of Milan Fashion Week, was held at the Allianz Cloud Arena in front of a crowd of nearly 5,000, including Normani, Julia Fox, Evan Mock, Skepta and more. 

    'I wanted to open Diesel up to the public, for people who may never have been to a fashion show before,' said creative director, Glenn Martens.

    Glenn added: 'They deserve a spectacle, so we’ve broken the record for the biggest inflatable sculpture in the world. It’s what I believe about the fashion and the state of mind - everybody can be part of Diesel.'

    Models in the show walked around the sensual inflatable sculpture, a depiction of two lovers whose limbs were entangled in one another.

    From the early days of its founding in 1978 by Renzo Rosso, Diesel has been an innovative international brand and a leader in the category.

    At the forefront of experimentation, cultural subversion and inclusivity is in its DNA and this 21st century Diesel reboot has only just begun.

    Denim devoré is created by weaving denim onto a transparent base, like the devoré corset waist of low-slung denim pants seen on the catwalk.

    The highly innovative technique of solarising left utility pants with supersized pockets and other garments with an edgy 'left out in the sun' look. 

    Scans of distressed denim are featured on second-skin trompe-l’oeil prints. 

    For the perfect juxtaposition, acid floral slip dresses are trimmed with lace, as are tank minidresses with frill at the neck and arm.

    Beyond apparel, the brand’s collections include accessories and a wide range of lifestyle collaborations.
