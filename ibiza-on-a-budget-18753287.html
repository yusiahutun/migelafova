    Ibiza on a budget: How to visit the White Isle in style

    Ibiza. Fun, beautiful - and bloomin' expensive. Yep, at around 25 for a vodka and Red Bull, 37 to get into a club and as much as a tenner for a mini bottle of water, a trip to the White Isle can make you poor.

    01 Jan 0001, 00:00

    Ibiza. Fun, beautiful - and bloomin' expensive.

    Yep, at around £25 for a vodka and Red Bull, £37 to get into a club and as much as a tenner for a mini bottle of water, a trip to the White Isle can make you poor.

    However there are ways around this island’s expensive tastes.

    As its final, and best, month of the season rocks around, you can pop over there and make it one savvy September break.

    STAY AND PLAY

    Book yourself into the brand spanking new THB Ocean Beach Hotel on the nice side of San Antonio (oceanbeachibiza.com).

    Super luxe, it’s all white washed walls, funky lobby with LED lights and art work scattered about the place.

    The suites - you don’t just get rooms here - have cute terraces, big bathrooms and absolutely massive beds.

    Down by the bright turquoise pool you’ll find massive sun beds decorated with canopies, fresh orange towels and gold cushions.

    Here you will be tended to by hot waiters in white shorts and T-shirts, bringing everything from iced coffee to sushi to your lounger.

    Best of all this celeb haunt, where the likes of Michelle Keegan, Mark Wright and Ashley Roberts have stayed, overlooks the famous Ocean Beach Ibiza (oceanbeachibiza.com). Sister to the Marbella club, this is cooler thanks to the top DJS, massive round pool beds and fun crowd.

    There are still Spray Parties, with champagne doing the rounds on Sundays, and a celeb attendee list including Frankie Sandford, Rosie Hungtington Whiteley and Laura Whitmore.

    Make sure you dress to impress.

    And the best thing about this set up? There is a secret stairway from the hotel to the beach club, and as a resident of THB Ocean Beach Hotel you get access for free, swerving the £27 entry fee (and extra to bag a bed).

    On our trip we popped there every day for pre-night out drinks, a cheeky dip while the DJs played and of course to eye up the buff boys in vests, who love posing around the pool.

    Prices for Suites at THB Ocean Beach Hotel start from £127 per room, per night based on two sharing.

    So for a luxe pad, A-list pool, beach club partying on tap and a complimentary bottle of cava upon arrival you only have to pay £63 per person.

    For Ibiza, that is a steal.

    THE SUN SET

    San Antonio may not be the most showbiz of Ibiza spots, but if you dodge the main muddle of Irish pubs and bar reps and head for the Sunset Strip, you will be in for a treat.

    A 15-minute stroll from THB Ocean Beach Hotel, it’s home to old school White Isle hangouts like Café Del Mar and Café Mambo.

    You can pop into the latter for a nice tea – we tucked into steak and chips, washed down with cava, for £30 per person – or soak up the atmosphere for pretty much free.

    How? Well, at the little window of Café Mambo (cafemamboibiza.com), at around 9pm a top DJ will pop up to play a mini set.

    Duke Dumont rocked out some funky house during our session, and other big names who take to the decks here include Fat Boy Slim and Erick Morillo.

    There are resident players too and of course they all rock out the tunes in a suitably loud fashion.

    And all this can be heard from the rocky beach bellow the decks of the café.

    So pop to the local shop, pick up four beers for a tenner and then hang out below deck.

    Of course that is just the warm up because when you're in Ibiza you have to go "out out".

    BIG NIGHT OUT

    Ticket entry is super pricey compared to what we pay in the UK but if you work it out at cost per hour of dancing – you tend to go to the big clubs for 1am and don’t leave until at least 6am – it is good value.

    And bagging your tickets before you fly can cut costs too.

    Try Ibizadiscoticket.com.

    Our top three Ibiza nights are:

    1. Ants at Ushuaïa (ushuaiaibiza.com) - every Saturday.

    Expect to pay around £30 for an open air extravaganza of mischief.

    The huge stage rocks over the pool complete with dancers in all in one black jump suits and a fun crowd who dance under the stars until midnight.

    It’s the perfect warm-up before heading to a bigger club.

    2. Pure Pacha at Pacha (pacha.com) – every Saturday.

    This will cost you about £35 to get in.

    But it’s worth every cent.

    Big names like Bob Sinclair, Martin Solveig and Danny Howard take over the main room.

    You can hang out on the terrace with immense views over Ibiza town and marina.

    Plus, if you need a bit of a house or techno break, slip into the side room for retro r’n’b, garage and hip hop.

    3. We Love…Sundays At Space (welove-music.com) – every Sunday.

    Disclosure, Groove Armada, Rudimental… The impressive line-up list goes on.

    You cannot miss this Ibiza night out.

    Blown up smiley faces bounce from the ceilings, there’s an open air rooftop that’s perfect for supping beers and snogging on, and a maze of massive dance rooms.

    You can't fail to have a bangin' time.

    Getting there:

    Jet off to Ibiza from UK airports with easyJet this September, with prices from £200 return.

    See easyjet.com.
