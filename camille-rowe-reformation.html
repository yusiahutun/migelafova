    Camille Rowe on her new Reformation collection

    &quot;I love clothes and think about them all the time,&quot; says Camille Rowe, who has just taken her love of fashion to the next level with her new capsule collection for the Los Angeles-based label Reformation.

    25 Mar 2024, 00:00

    "I love clothes and think about them all the time," says Camille Rowe, who has just taken her love of fashion to the next level with her new capsule collection for the Los Angeles-based label Reformation.

    Described as "made for 'It' girls by an 'It' girl," the capsule is filled with desirable autumn/winter staples and party pieces (and, even better, is now heavily discounted in the pre-Christmas sales). The French model and actress has been busy promoting the collection for the past few weeks – and we caught up with her as she hosted a dinner in London to celebrate the launch.

    Antony Jones

    "Fashion is such a beautiful form of expression and it's an amazing way to present yourself to others and the world," she told us, before sharing one of her favourite pieces of style advice: "A heel is always a good idea".

    Below, see what Rowe had to say about being inspired by Tom Ford's Gucci, why comfort is sexy and when she feels most beautiful.

    Please talk us through your look – what drew you to this particular outfit?

    "It's funny, when we were in Los Angeles doing the final editing to the collection, it wasn't initially my favourite dress. It is so special and I love the idea of a naked dress with a bit of shine, but I always thought it wouldn't work with my colouring. We ended up shooting it for the campaign and that's when I knew it was the one for the Reformation dinner. As it was the first view of the collection being presented to the world, it made so much sense to wear it."

    Moeez Ali for Reformation

    How do you decide which brands you want to work with?

    "I've been working with Reformation for a long time through modelling, as well as shopping there since I first moved to New York when the store had a real vintage focus. I later worked with the brand on a collection in 2014, so I have some experience under my belt. I've been friends with the Reformation girls for a long time, so they asked me to do a capsule collection – which was really exciting as I've never done anything like that. Usually, brands come to me on projects and I just go with what feels right."

    What makes Reformation such a great fit for you and your personal style?

    "I love vintage, so Reformation is a perfect fit for me; they started out working with vintage and have a strong sustainability aspect. I think it's amazing that they're such pioneers of sustainability in fashion, as well as having a strong sense of self. We all know who the Ref girl is: it's girly without being too girly. It just works."

    Moeez Ali for Reformation

    Can you tell us a little about the inspiration behind your collection?

    "It began with going through my own clothes and looking at the pieces I've held on to for so long – my wardrobe is a lot of vintage so it all started from there – as well as reference images. We looked at a lot of Nineties style and vintage designer collections, in particular Tom Ford for Gucci. I wanted it to feel sexy. I don't know what it is, perhaps it's following the pandemic, but I want to dress up whenever I can and show more skin. I knew I wanted to take the collection in that direction and Tom Ford really knows how to design for women, so that was a big inspiration. We used that as the base and reimagined it with a Ref touch."

    How involved do you tend to be with the styling process?

    "Styling is my favourite. I love clothes and think about them all the time. I work with [celebrity stylist] Danielle Goldberg for events. She helps me reach out to brands and knows my style so well. We are great friends, so can have conversations about who I want to be at a particular event; she really shares my vision. She's so brilliant and we've worked together for a long time. It's a real collaboration between the two of us."

    Moeez Ali for Reformation

    What kind of fashion makes you feel your most confident?

    "Comfort is key and makes you feel sexy. A heel is always a good idea – it's a basic add-on to your outfit that makes all the difference and makes you carry yourself differently. You walk and all of a sudden you feel like you're in a movie or something; that's the person you become when wearing heels. Also, in all honesty, there's nothing better than a button-down shirt with that extra button open. It's the sexiest outfit in the world in my opinion."

    Moeez Ali for Reformation

    How has your style evolved over the years?

    "Barely. My father would wear vintage Levi's and boots when we were growing up, and I haven't strayed too far from the way he dressed when I was 10 years old. I just opt for a little more of a feminine edge. I'd like to say I take more risks now, but I've always stuck to the same kind of style, which depends largely on how I'm feeling – so I always revert back to what I know when I don't want to think about clothes. I get more dressed up in the evening now too and have thought more about jewellery over the years and incorporating that has made a big difference."

    What has been your most memorable fashion moment in recent years and why?

    "It has to be the Celine dress I wore to the Vanity Fair Oscars party. It was a green, completely beaded gown that felt really different for me. I know I've talked about comfort being important, but that dress was the most uncomfortable dress in the world. It was so heavy it cut my shoulder! It was very sexy for me, with a deep plunge at the front, there was a lot of boob and it was hard to walk in. Yet, it was one of the most stunning gowns I had ever worn. It was talked-about and people were excited by it, so I'm glad I took that risk."

    Whose style have you always admired and why?

    "My father for sure. He's always been a kid at heart – now he's getting older he's still the same cool Frenchman. He's usually wearing cowboy boots and great Levi's denim with amazing Hawaiian shirts or a cashmere jumper. He mixes colour so well and knows the importance of creating a good base for your outfit."

    What is your ideal getting-ready set-up?

    "Good lighting! When I'm doing hair and make-up, I always tend to work with the same people as there's a real friendship and trust built there. I always want to work with people I know who have good energy. I love listening to the Rolling Stones and having a glass of wine; getting ready should be fun and sometimes we have so much fun we don't even want to go to the event we're getting ready for. Getting ready is the best part."

    Moeez Ali for Reformation

    Moeez Ali for Reformation

    When do you feel most beautiful?

    "When the company and conversation are right. I love being with my best girlfriends; some I've grown up with and have known since birth as our parents were friends, some I went to school with and I've known some since I was a little older. I'm so proud of all my best friends – everyone is doing so well in different fields. When we all get together, everyone is just so beautiful and smart in their own way. It's so empowering to be surrounded by these women. It may sound cheesy, but that's what makes me feel my most beautiful."

    Fashion has a reputation for being frivolous, but why does what we wear matter?

    "Fashion is such a beautiful form of expression and it's an amazing way to present yourself to others and the world. It acts as an extra layer of your personality, [a visual indication] of what you're about or how you're feeling on a particular day. Fashion is an extension of who you are. It doesn't matter if you're not that into it, but it can never be disregarded completely as it's part of our everyday. If you are really into fashion, you can use it as an art form. It's so beautiful to have a creative mind and to support designers that do as well."

    Moeez Ali for Reformation
