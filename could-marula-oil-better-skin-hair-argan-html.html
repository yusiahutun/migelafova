    13 Feb 2024, 00:00

    When argan oil burst onto the scene a few years ago it caused a stir in the beauty world, with fans raving about its many benefits for hair and skin.

    But now there's a new oil on the market that some skin care experts believe may be even better for you. Marula is fast becoming a rival to argan, after earning itself a reputation as a 'miracle oil' thanks to its anti-aging and skin hydration properties.

    In fact, beauty brand Marula Beauty Oil cites a study that suggests that marula contains more fatty acids, higher levels of antioxidants and boasts better absorption and penetration, making it the ideal choice for hydration, anti-aging and healing.

    Marula is fast becoming a rival to argan, after earning itself a reputation as a 'miracle oil' thanks to its anti-aging and skin hydration properties

    It is also an ethical choice for consumers since many brands, including Marula Beauty Oil, focus on sustainable farming when they harvest the marula nut in southern Africa, and support local women. 

    But to make sure you're making the right choice by switching your argan for marula, we spoke to skin care experts Dr Marnie Nussbaum and Terri Lawton to assess the benefits of each oil and determine which will work best for your individual complexion and needs. 

    Best for your skin type  

    Both oils come from Africa, with marula hailing from the southern region and argan originating in Morocco. Holistic skin care therapist Terri Lawton, who runs a practice in Los Angeles, points out that they actually have very similar ingredients, and the differences come from the ratios of these ingredients.

    Ms Lawton explains: 'What differs between the two is besides the concentration levels of linoleic acid, oleic acids, UV protection, [and] antioxidants.'

    'Argan oil has a high concentration of lightweight linoleic acid and is therefore better when used on oilier skin types or acne-prone skin,' Dr Marnie Nussbaum, an NYC-based board-certified dermatologist, adds.

    'Marula oil consists of a high concentration of heavier-weight oleic acid, which is super hydrating and therefore better for dry skin.' She also points out that it contains a high percentage of essential fatty acids, which makes it a great moisturizer.

    Beauty brand Marula Beauty Oil (right) cites a study that suggests that marula contains more fatty acids, higher levels of antioxidants and better absorption and penetration than argan

    Ms Lawton agrees that argan oil is good for oily skin. 'Argan oil is great for acne-prone skin as it won't clog skin typically,' she says.

    'Marula oil is best for dryness and for moisture barrier repair,' she adds. 'Some skins need the barrier to be repaired in order to keep the moisture in and stay healthy.' 

    Best for anti-aging

    Both argan and marula have anti-aging properties, but Ms Lawton says that the best product to use depends on your particular complexion.

    WHICH MARULA OIL SHOULD I USE? 

    As with any beauty product, it's important to consider where and how marula oil is sourced to ensure that not only are you getting the best product, but that the oil is sustainability farmed.

    Marula Pure Beauty Oil is one such oil. The company wild harvests marula fruit in Africa using an eco-friendly process that not only protects the trees but also supports local women.

    The brand works with women across Africa to wild harvest the fallen marula fruit, hand crack the nuts, and then carefully select the purest blonde kernels to be cold pressed to create the oil.  

    Marula Pure Beauty Oil subsidizes the families' earnings and builds daycare centers and schools in key areas, helping to empower these businesswomen to become a driving force in the success of their communities. 

    When the marula is collected, only the fallen fruit is taken rather than the hanging strands, which helps to protect the trees.

    'It is not all about what some else is using and having great results with, but rather connecting with your own skin and thinking about what is needed,' she explains. 

    'If the barrier of the skin is very stressed, you need to start with repairing the surface as it is the first skin structure designed to protect and heal.'

    In this case she believes marula may be the best choice as it is so effective at moisture barrier repair.

    Dr Nussbaum suggests that marula may have the edge when it comes to anti-aging, thanks to its moisturizing properties. 

    She says: 'Both are effective at anti-aging, however marula oil will be slightly more hydrating to plump the skin and minimize fine lines and wrinkles.'

    Best for your hair  

    According to Ms Lawton, the most suitable oil to use on your tresses depends on your hair type, as marula and argan have different benefits.

    'Marula oil is best used on hair that is very dry and frizzy or has cuticle damage as it helps moisturize and seal the cuticle of the hair,' she explains.

    'Argan oil is best for thinner hair, [and] hair that needs more volume as it penetrates deeper than marula typically.' 

    Best for skin protection 

    Dr Nussbaum says that both oils will provide some UV protection, but marula oil has a higher antioxidant count than argan and therefore is better at combating free radical damage from the sun's rays.

    She explains: 'Antioxidants neutralize free radical damage that can lead to skin cancer and they help to decrease the signs of skin aging by preventing the breakdown of collagen and elastin.'
