    Wealthy wife embroiled in divorce case demands $5MILLION a year

    A wealthy ex-wife is demanding a huge divorce settlement from her former husband so that she can maintain her lavish lifestyle. Richard and Alicia Stephenson have been wrangling for seven years over the amount of maintenance she is due, and over her possible stake in several other assets.

    03 Apr 2024, 00:00

    A wealthy ex-wife is demanding a huge divorce settlement from her former husband so that she can maintain her lavish lifestyle. 

    Richard and Alicia Stephenson have been wrangling for seven years over the amount of maintenance she is due, and over her possible stake in several other assets.

    A trial is currently underway to determine how much she will receive, with Mrs Stephenson seeking $5million a year from her ex-husband.

    Alicia Stephenson is demanding a settlement of $5million a year after divorcing from her multimillionaire  ex-husband

    Cathleen Belmonte Newman, a certified divorce financial analyst, said she had carried out a 'lifestyle analysis', which determined that Stephenson would need $433,991 'net' in monthly maintenance to maintain the standard of living she had become accustomed to.

    Her ex-husband is the multimillionaire founder and chairman of the privately owned Cancer Treatment Centers of America hospital network.

    Mrs Stephenson signed a prenuptial agreement before the couple married in 1991, but the document said that monthly maintenance would be up for negotiation if the marriage lasted more than seven years.  

    Newman testified that Stephenson will need approximately $9 million total to buy two properties - a condominium in Chicago and a house in Naples, Florida, the Chicago Tribune reports.

    The analysis said monthly mortgage payments and taxes would come in at around $30,000 a month for the Chicago condo, with an extra $2,800 or so needed to pay for assessments, while the home in Naples would cost about the same. 

    Richard Stephenson is the founder and chairman of the privately owned Cancer Treatment Centers of America hospital network

    She is looking for a further $23,200 a month to rent a waterfront rental property in Michigan, to use as a vacation home from May to September. 

    Stephenson is also seeking more than $100,000 per month to go towards entertainment, which would include visits to the Sundance Film Festival, Napa Valley and Fashion Week in California, and trips on private jets. 

    She is also seeking $1,070 to maintain four motorcycles,  as well as funds for two boats and a four-person watercraft.

    Her testimony said she was accustomed to a full social life during her marriage, in which she regularly ate out, and attended concerts, Broadway plays and sporting events.

    The trial in McHenry County heard that while they were together the couple had owned houses in Michigan, Barrington Hills, the Virgin Islands, Colorado and Tennessee.  

    Richard Stephenson's attorney, David Grund, is disputing the claim, saying the report was based solely on Alicia Stephenson's 'desires and wants.'

    The hearing is now in its third week, and lawyers have asked the judge for an additional two weeks to complete the trial.
