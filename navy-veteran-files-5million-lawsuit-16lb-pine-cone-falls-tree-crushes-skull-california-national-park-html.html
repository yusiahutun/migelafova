    San Francisco man sues after a PINE CONE falls on him in California national park

    21 Apr 2024, 00:00

    A Navy veteran has filed a $5million lawsuit after a giant pine cone apparently fell out of a tree and crushed his skull in a California national park, leaving him with life-changing injuries.

    Sean Mace, 50, who served for his country for two years in the 1980s, was reading a book under a non-native pine tree in San Francisco Maritime National Historical Park when he was hit last October.

    His lawyer says Mace had been waiting to watch a Blue Angels air show when a '16-pound pine cone' fell out of the tree and smashed into his skull, causing severe swelling and a brain hemorrhage.

    Lawsuit: Sean Mace (left) of Washington state, has filed a $5million lawsuit after a giant pine cone (similar to these ones, pictured right) apparently fell out of a tree and crushed his skull in a California national park

    He was rushed to hospital, where he had two emergency surgeries to relieve pressure on his brain.

    He is awaiting a third operation - but has been left with permanent brain damage, memory loss, depression and 'a tremendous degree of anxiety', his attorney Scott Johnson told NBC.

    Mace also 'can't hold down' a job and has hundreds of thousands of dollars in medical bills to pay.

    Mace is suing the U.S. Government, the National Park Service, the Department of the Interior and the San Francisco national park for $5million, the San Francisco Chronicle reports.

    'This guy has an irreversible brain injury,' Mr Johnson said of his client. 'He’s in constant fear of getting hit on the head. It’s like a bowling ball falling from the sky and hitting you on the head.'

    In the wake of the incident, park officials have added warning signs (above), reading: 'Danger: Giant seed pod falling from tree' with an image of a man and a pine cone

    He added that a portion of his client's skull had to be removed during surgery to save his life.

    Mr Mace was reportedly lying down in the park on October 12 last year, reading a book and napping and waiting for the air show to begin, when the pine cone - also known as a 'seed pod' - plummeted from the coniferous Araucaria bidwillii tree.

    It landed on his head, knocking him unconscious, according to the lawsuit filed in the San Francisco federal court on September 4.

    Mr Johnson alleges that there were no warning signs about falling pine cones in the park - and that officials should have known about the danger and prevented visitors from resting under the trees. 

    Mace is suing the U.S. Government, the National Park Service, the Department of the Interior and the San Francisco national park (pictured)

    'First and foremost, the Park Service needs to do something to make sure this never happens again,' he said. 'This park is full of tourists and schoolchildren. Something needs to change.'

    In the wake of the incident, the park fenced off the grove, NBC reports.

    Officials also added signs warning: 'Danger: Giant seed pod falling from tree.'

    Mr Mace now lives in Washington state, where he is being cared for by loved ones. 

    Representatives from the U.S. attorney's office and the park did not return requests for a comment.
