    If you’ve ever indulged in a balayage colour or enlisted a stylist to help you go blonde, you’ve likely already heard of Olaplex – the strengthening treatment that can bring even the most intensely bleached hair back from the brink. Since hitting the professional market seven years ago, it has become an essential component of so many salons’ colour processes, completely revolutionising the way we bleach, treat and tone our hair.

    Such was the popularity of this bond-strengthening (more on that later) treatment, that the brand quickly moved into the at-home product arena, launching a growing collection of shampoos, conditioners, and leave-in treatments.

    The at-home routine quickly garnered a loyal A-list fan base too, with everyone from Jennifer Lopez to Kim Kardashian crediting it for keeping their hair in top condition through constant colour changes. Even Rosie Huntington-Whiteley swears by it for counteracting the colour and heat damage her hair is subjected to.

Since using Olaplex over the last couple of years I’ve found that it really does hydrate my hair, strengthen it, and repair the ends and the brittle parts.”

    Today, there's an Olaplex product to slot into pretty much any part of your routine, but the brand's most recent launch has really piqued our interest. This month, Olaplex moves further into the styling arena with the launch of No.4 D Clean Detox Dry Shampoo – an oil-absorber that's quite unlike the others.

    If you're a dry shampoo user, you're likely familiar with the chalky residue almost every application delivers. In short, most dry shampoos work by using starch to physically soak up excess oil, hence the white flecks. Olaplex's offering, however, uses 'ultra-fine' starch particles that are genuinely invisible on every hair colour. The product itself is watery rather than powdery, and makes roots feels properly refreshed. If you use dry shampoo to add volume, this one won't be for you, but for stretching out that bouncy blow-dry, it's just the thing.

    How does Olaplex work?

    Simply speaking, Olaplex formulas penetrate each hair strand (rather than slipping over the surface) to repair the disulphide bonds that become damaged as a result of bleaching, heat treatments, overzealous straightening, or simply heavy-handed brushing. By working to improve the structural integrity of each hair fibre, it goes further than a traditional hair mask, helping to reverse damage rather than simply glossing over it.

    How to use Olaplex

    The original Olaplex formula, confusingly named No. 3 Hair Perfector, is often misconstrued as a conditioner, but it’s actually designed to be used before shampoo as a pre-wash intensive treatment. The process is a little more lengthy than your usual deep-conditioning treatment, but the results are worth putting time aside for once a fortnight.

    Olaplex No.3 should be applied to damp (not dripping wet hair) before shampooing. Apply it across the whole of your head, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends, and leave it to work for a minimum of 10 minutes. Then, shampoo and condition as usual: you’ll likely notice smoother lengths and better shine from the first use, while the reparative benefits will accumulate the more you use it.

    As the brand continues to expand rapidly, you now have plenty of other options when it comes to getting your Olaplex fix. Olaplex No.0 is, as the name suggests, the first product you should reach for come wash-day. Designed to be used once a week, the milky liquid should be applied to dry hair before washing and left to work for 10 minutes. Water-weight and easy to rinse out, this is a particularly good formula for those with fine, flyaway hair. To intensify the results, the brand advises applying No.3 Hair Protector over the top, although we find both work perfectly well independently, so don't feel obliged to purchase both.

    If you're looking to repair significant damage, the rest of the line-up is also worth a look. The Bond Maintenance Shampoo and Conditioner work together to cleanse without stripping – for blondes, there's even a purple toning formula – while the leave-in Bond Smoother works to reduce frizz and enhance manageability. For hair in need of serious damage limitation, the highly intensive Bonding Oil is your lifeline: applied (with an extremely light hand) to damp hair, it'll protect from heat styling while strengthening frazzled ends.

    Finally, the Bond Repair Moisture Mask is one that all hair types are bound to love: concentrated and colour-safe, this weekly intensive mask delivers moisture and smoothness without compromising body, which is where many lesser formulas tend to falter.
