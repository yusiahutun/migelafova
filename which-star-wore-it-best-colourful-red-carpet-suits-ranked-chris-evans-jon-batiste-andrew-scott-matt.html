    While there’s nothing wrong with a timeless, classic tux, stylists should encourage their celebrity clients to have some fun on the red carpet and shun the cookie-cutter approach typical of men’s evening looks.

    Amid a sea of black this award season, a few men stood out for their more colourful choices. A splash of colour really makes a statement and you don’t have to overdo it: just keep things tailored and sharp and you’ll still look elegant but not boring. After all, you want to stand next to the glamorous ladies who pull out all the stops on the red carpet and not feel invisible in monochrome.

    14 best Oscars red carpet looks, from Ariana’s pink to Gosling’s ‘Kenergy’

    On Oscar night, singer Jon Batiste looked super chic in a burgundy wool blazer with matching wide-leg pants and a shirt with stand-up collar from Italian menswear label Zegna.

    The actor who really made a bold statement, however, was Marvel star Chris Evans, who showed up to the Vanity Fair Oscar Party in a custom double-breasted suit with a shirt and silk tie from Dolce & Gabbana. The fiery red of the suit was very striking and the cut did justice to his broad frame.

    Someone who has made a few colourful statements throughout awards season is Matt Bomer, who at the 81st Golden Globe Awards in January wore a bespoke jewel-tone silk dinner jacket, black evening trousers and ecru silk evening shirt from Rome-based label Brioni.

    Who is Kendall Jenner’s glamorous lookalike friend, Lauren Perez?

    Finally, for a more casual take on the trend, you can find some style inspiration in Andrew Scott, who had his first break in British show Fleabag and most recently made headlines for his moving role in All of Us Strangers. At the Film Independent Spirit Awards in February, Scott wore a banana-yellow custom suit from Etro and paired it with a sheer white polo shirt.
